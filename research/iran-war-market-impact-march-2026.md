# Iran War — Impact on Indian Markets (March 2026)

Date: 2026-03-25

## Event Summary

US-Israel war on Iran. Strait of Hormuz shut to tanker traffic — largest supply disruption in oil market history (IEA). Brent crude swung from ~$70 pre-war to $119.50 peak, trading ~$103 as of March 24. Trump announced 5-day pause on strikes against Iran energy infrastructure on March 23.

## Sector Rotation

**Money flowing INTO (relative strength):**
- Energy / Oil PSUs (ONGC, Oil India) — direct crude beneficiaries
- Power & Renewables — defensive + policy tailwind
- Telecom — non-cyclical, domestic demand
- Capital Goods — L&T +5.25% on ceasefire rally

**Money flowing OUT OF:**
- Auto — crude spike = input cost + consumer sentiment hit
- Realty — rate cut cycle now uncertain with $100+ oil
- BFSI — heaviest FII selling concentration
- IT — global slowdown fears

## India-Specific Vulnerability

India imports 85% of crude oil. $100+ Brent directly hits:
- Current account deficit widens
- Rupee weakens (erodes FII dollar-term returns, accelerating exits)
- RBI rate cut expectations pushed out
- Corporate margins compress (especially OMCs, airlines, auto)

## Key Takeaway

This is a war-driven, FII-exit driven correction — not a fundamental breakdown. Indian corporate earnings, credit growth, and domestic consumption remain intact. Recovery depends on ceasefire outcome and crude normalization below $95.
