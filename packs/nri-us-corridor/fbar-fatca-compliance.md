# FBAR & FATCA Compliance for US NRIs
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: FinCEN BSA, IRC Section 6038D (FATCA)
**Target agent**: nri-advisor

## FBAR (FinCEN 114)

### Who Must File
Any US person (citizen, green card holder, tax resident) with financial interest in or signature authority over foreign financial accounts, if the aggregate value of ALL foreign accounts exceeded **$10,000 at any time** during the calendar year.

### What to Report
- Bank accounts (NRE, NRO, savings, current, FD)
- Demat accounts holding securities
- Mutual fund accounts
- PPF accounts
- NPS accounts
- Insurance policies with cash value
- Any other financial account outside the US

### Key Rules
- **Filing deadline**: April 15 (auto-extension to October 15)
- **Filed electronically** via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System
- **Penalty for non-filing**: Up to $10,000 per violation (non-willful), or greater of $100,000 or 50% of account balance (willful)
- **6-year record retention** required
- Report the maximum balance during the year for each account
- Convert INR to USD using Treasury year-end rate

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to include NRE FDs (they're still foreign accounts)
- Not reporting joint accounts (each joint holder must file separately)
- Missing PPF/NPS (yes, these are reportable financial accounts)
- Using wrong exchange rate (must use Treasury rate, not market rate)

## FATCA (Form 8938)

### Who Must File (Specified Foreign Financial Assets)
| Filing Status | Threshold (US resident) | Threshold (Foreign resident) |
|--------------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Single | >$50K year-end or >$75K during year | >$200K year-end or >$300K during year |
| Married filing jointly | >$100K year-end or >$150K during year | >$400K year-end or >$600K during year |

### What to Report
Everything in FBAR, PLUS:
- Stock or securities issued by non-US entity (Indian shares held outside US brokerage)
- Financial instruments or contracts with non-US counterparty
- Interest in foreign entity (e.g., partnership, trust)
- Indian real estate held through an entity (not directly)

### Key Differences from FBAR
| Feature | FBAR | FATCA (Form 8938) |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| Filed with | FinCEN | IRS (with tax return) |
| Threshold | $10,000 aggregate | $50,000+ (varies) |
| Real estate | Not reportable | Not if held directly |
| Penalty | $10K-$100K+ | $10K + continuation penalty |
| Statute of limitations | 6 years | 3 years (6 if >$5K omission) |

## Indian Account Reporting to US (Automatic)

Under the India-US FATCA IGA (Intergovernmental Agreement), Indian financial institutions automatically report US persons' accounts to the IRS. This means:
- Your Indian bank/broker reports your account details to Indian tax authority
- Indian tax authority shares with IRS under FATCA IGA
- IRS can match against your filed returns
- **Non-compliance is increasingly detectable**

## Compliance Checklist for US NRIs

1. [ ] File FBAR by April 15 (extension to Oct 15)
2. [ ] File Form 8938 with tax return if thresholds met
3. [ ] Report NRE FD interest on US return (even though tax-free in India)
4. [ ] Report Indian rental income on US return
5. [ ] Report Indian capital gains on US return
6. [ ] Claim Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116) for Indian taxes paid
7. [ ] Report gifts received from India >$100K (Form 3520)
8. [ ] Avoid Indian MFs (PFIC reporting — Form 8621 — is extremely burdensome)
9. [ ] Keep records for 6 years minimum
10. [ ] Use same exchange rates consistently (Treasury FY-end rate for FBAR)
