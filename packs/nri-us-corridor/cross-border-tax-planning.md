# Cross-Border Tax Planning for US NRIs
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: Practitioner guidance, India-US DTAA
**Target agent**: nri-advisor

## Investment Strategy by Tax Efficiency

### Best for US NRIs (Tax-Efficient)
1. **Indian direct equity** via NRE-linked demat: LTCG 12.5% in India, claim FTC in US. No PFIC issue.
2. **NRE Fixed Deposits**: Tax-free in India, taxable in US at ordinary rates. Good for INR exposure.
3. **Sovereign Gold Bonds**: Tax-free LTCG at maturity (8yr) in India. Taxable in US.
4. **US-listed India ETFs** (INDA, SMIN, INDY): No PFIC, no India filing. Taxed under US rules only.
5. **Real estate**: LTCG 12.5% in India, FTC in US. Good for long-term hold.

### Avoid (Tax-Inefficient)
1. **Indian mutual funds**: PFIC classification makes US taxation punitive (excess distribution method or QEF/MTM — all complex and unfavorable)
2. **Indian insurance products**: Complex US tax treatment, may be PFIC
3. **PPF new contributions**: Can't contribute as NRI (account continues but no new deposits). Interest taxable in US.
4. **Small savings schemes**: NRIs can't open new accounts

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Across Borders

- **In India**: Sell loss-making Indian stocks before March 31. Book loss. Set off against gains.
- **In US**: Sell loss-making US investments before December 31. Harvest up to $3,000 against ordinary income.
- **Coordination**: Ensure losses in one country don't create phantom income in the other due to FX fluctuation.

## Currency Considerations

- NRE deposits benefit when INR depreciates vs USD (larger USD value on repatriation)
- Indian equity returns must account for INR/USD movement for true USD returns
- Hedge only if exposure is >20% of net worth (hedging cost typically 3-4% p.a.)

## Repatriation Planning

### From NRE Account
- Freely repatriable — no limit
- No tax in India on NRE FD interest
- Simply wire to US bank account

### From NRO Account
- Limited to $1M per financial year (after taxes)
- Need: Form 15CA (online filing) + Form 15CB (CA certificate)
- TDS already deducted on NRO income
- Sources: rental income, dividends, interest, capital gains proceeds
- Process: Obtain CA certificate → File 15CA online → Bank processes wire

### Property Sale Proceeds
- TDS: 12.5% for LTCG (>24 months) or slab rate for STCG
- Can repatriate via NRO after TDS
- Buyer must deduct TDS and deposit with govt
- Seller can apply for lower TDS certificate (Section 197) if actual tax is lower

## Returning to India (Becoming Resident)

When a US NRI moves back to India:
1. **RNOR status**: First 2-3 years, foreign income not taxable in India
2. **Move investments during RNOR**: Transfer US investments without Indian tax
3. **Convert NRE to resident account**: NRE FDs can continue till maturity at NRE rates
4. **PFIC exit**: Sell Indian MFs before becoming US non-resident (if holding any)
5. **401k/IRA**: Keep in US, withdraw after retirement. India taxes under DTAA.
6. **US pension/Social Security**: Taxable in India as resident, claim DTAA credit for any US tax

## Annual Tax Filing Requirements

| Return | Where | Deadline | What to Report |
|--------|-------|----------|----------------|
| ITR (India) | India IT dept | July 31 | Indian income, capital gains, property |
| Form 1040 (US) | IRS | April 15 | Worldwide income including India |
| FBAR | FinCEN | April 15 (ext Oct) | All Indian financial accounts |
| Form 8938 | IRS (with 1040) | April 15 | If thresholds met |
| Form 1116 | IRS (with 1040) | April 15 | Foreign tax credit claim |
