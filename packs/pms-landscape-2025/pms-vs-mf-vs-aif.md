# PMS vs Mutual Funds vs AIFs — Decision Framework
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: SEBI regulations, industry comparison
**Target agent**: pms-analyst

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Mutual Fund | PMS | AIF Cat II | AIF Cat III |
|---------|------------|-----|-----------|-------------|
| Minimum | Rs 100 (SIP) | Rs 50L | Rs 1 Cr | Rs 1 Cr |
| Regulation | SEBI MF | SEBI PMS | SEBI AIF | SEBI AIF |
| Ownership | Units | Direct shares | Fund units | Fund units |
| Customization | None | High | None | None |
| Tax | Fund-level (LTCG/STCG) | Individual transactions | Pass-through | Fund-level |
| Expense ratio | 0.1-2.25% | 1-3%+ | 1-2% + carry | 1-2% + carry |
| Liquidity | T+2 (equity) | T+3 to T+7 | Lock-in (3yr+) | Lock-in (varies) |
| Transparency | Monthly factsheet | Real-time portfolio | Quarterly | Quarterly |
| Number of investors | Unlimited | Per account | Max 1000 | Max 1000 |

## Decision Matrix

### Choose Mutual Funds When:
- Investment amount < Rs 50L
- Want simplicity and liquidity
- Passive/index strategy preferred
- Don't need customization
- Want SIP/STP flexibility
- Tax simplicity (single redemption transaction)

### Choose PMS When:
- Investment amount Rs 50L - 5Cr
- Want concentrated, high-conviction portfolio
- Need customization (exclude sectors/stocks)
- Want tax-lot optimization (specific lot selling)
- Prefer direct stock ownership
- Willing to accept higher volatility for potential alpha
- Need portfolio-level capital gains management

### Choose AIF Cat II When:
- Investment amount > Rs 1Cr
- Want PE/VC/structured credit exposure
- Comfortable with 3-5 year lock-in
- Seeking differentiated return streams
- Tax-savvy (pass-through taxation benefits)

### Choose AIF Cat III When:
- Investment amount > Rs 1Cr
- Want long-short / hedge fund strategies
- Seeking absolute returns (not benchmark-relative)
- Comfortable with complex strategies (derivatives, arbitrage)
- Can handle potential for negative years

## Tax Implications Comparison

### Mutual Funds
- Equity LTCG: 12.5% above Rs 1.25L per year (all MF redemptions combined)
- Equity STCG: 20%
- Debt MF: Slab rate (no LTCG benefit since April 2023)
- Single transaction per redemption — simple

### PMS
- Each stock sale is a separate transaction
- LTCG/STCG calculated per stock lot (FIFO or specific identification)
- Higher churn = more STCG events = higher tax
- **Advantage**: Can specifically choose which lots to sell for tax optimization
- **Disadvantage**: High-churn PMS generates significant tax drag (3-5% annual drag possible)

### AIF Cat II (Pass-Through)
- Income passes through to investors
- Capital gains retain their character (LTCG stays LTCG)
- Business income may be passed through (taxed at slab)
- Investors file in their own ITR — can be complex

### AIF Cat III (Fund-Level)
- Taxed at fund level at maximum marginal rate (~42.7%)
- No pass-through benefit
- Returns are post-tax at fund level
- Investors receive post-tax distributions
- **Significant tax disadvantage** compared to PMS and Cat II

## Allocation Framework for HNI

| Portfolio Size | Recommended Mix |
|---------------|----------------|
| Rs 50L - 2Cr | 80% MF + 20% PMS |
| Rs 2Cr - 5Cr | 50% MF + 40% PMS + 10% AIF |
| Rs 5Cr - 25Cr | 30% MF + 40% PMS + 20% AIF + 10% International |
| Rs 25Cr+ | 20% MF + 30% PMS + 25% AIF + 15% International + 10% Alternates |

## Due Diligence Checklist

Before investing in any PMS/AIF:
1. [ ] Verify SEBI registration (sebi.gov.in)
2. [ ] Check 5-year track record (audited, not model portfolio)
3. [ ] Understand fee structure completely (including hidden costs + GST)
4. [ ] Read investment agreement fully
5. [ ] Verify high-water mark clause for performance fees
6. [ ] Check exit load schedule
7. [ ] Assess team stability (key person risk)
8. [ ] Compare after-tax returns (not just headline returns)
9. [ ] Understand portfolio concentration and turnover
10. [ ] Check if custodian is independent and reputed
