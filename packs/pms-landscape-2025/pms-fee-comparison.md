# PMS Fee Structures & Comparison
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: SEBI disclosures, PMS provider websites
**Target agent**: pms-analyst

## Fee Models

### Model 1: Fixed Only
- **Structure**: 1.5-2.5% of AUM per year
- **Example**: Rs 50L invested → Rs 75K-1.25L annual fee
- **Best when**: Returns are high (you don't share the upside)
- **Risk**: You pay even in losing years

### Model 2: Fixed + Performance
- **Structure**: 1-1.5% fixed + 15-20% of profits above hurdle
- **Example**: Rs 50L invested, 25% return = Rs 12.5L profit → Fixed Rs 62.5K + Performance Rs 1.5L (20% of Rs 7.5L above 10% hurdle) = Rs 2.12L
- **Most common model**
- **Best when**: Moderate confidence in manager
- **Risk**: High total cost in good years

### Model 3: Performance Only
- **Structure**: 0% fixed + 20-25% of profits above hurdle
- **Example**: Rs 50L, 25% return, 15% hurdle → 25% of Rs 5L = Rs 1.25L
- **Best when**: You want alignment — manager eats what they kill
- **Risk**: Manager may take excessive risk to earn fees

## Fee Impact on Returns (10-Year Simulation)

Starting corpus: Rs 50L, Gross return: 18% CAGR

| Fee Model | Annual Cost (avg) | 10-Year Net Corpus | Fee Drag |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|----------|
| 2% fixed only | Rs 1.5L (avg) | Rs 1.86 Cr | Rs 42L |
| 1% + 20% above 10% | Rs 2.6L (avg) | Rs 1.63 Cr | Rs 65L |
| 0% + 25% above 15% | Rs 1.0L (avg) | Rs 2.01 Cr | Rs 27L |
| Direct equity MF (0.5%) | Rs 0.5L (avg) | Rs 2.17 Cr | Rs 11L |

**Key insight**: Performance fees compound against you. A 1% + 20% structure can cost more than a 2% fixed-only structure in good years.

## Hidden Costs

| Cost | Typical Range | Often Disclosed? |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Brokerage | 0.03-0.1% per trade | Sometimes |
| STT | 0.1% on delivery | Rarely mentioned |
| Demat charges | Rs 500-2000/yr | Yes |
| GST on fees | 18% on management fee | Sometimes excluded from headline fee |
| Exit load | 1-3% if exit within 1-3 years | Yes |
| Account opening | Rs 0-5000 | Usually disclosed |

**Total hidden cost**: 0.3-0.5% additional annually

## SEBI PMS Fee Regulations

- No cap on fees (unlike mutual funds), but must be clearly disclosed
- Performance fee must have a high-water mark (can't charge again on same profits)
- Hurdle rate is optional but common
- Fee structure must be specified in client agreement
- Cannot charge entry load
- Exit load allowed (typically 1-3% reducing over time)

## When PMS Fees Are Justified

PMS is worth the fees if:
1. **Alpha generation**: Consistent 3-5%+ alpha over benchmark after fees
2. **Tax optimization**: PMS allows specific lot selling (not available in MF)
3. **Customization**: Can exclude sectors/stocks, accommodate ESG preferences
4. **Concentrated portfolio**: 15-25 stocks vs 50-80 in MF — higher conviction
5. **Direct stock ownership**: You own the shares (vs MF units) — no NAV delay

## When PMS Fees Are NOT Justified

Avoid PMS if:
1. Return profile mirrors a large-cap MF (just buy the MF at 0.5% cost)
2. High churn (>100% turnover) — generates excessive STCG
3. No clear investment philosophy differentiation
4. Manager AUM is too large to generate alpha in their chosen segment
5. Your investment amount is close to minimum (Rs 50L) — fee drag is proportionally high
