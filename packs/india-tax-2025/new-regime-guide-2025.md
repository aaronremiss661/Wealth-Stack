# New Tax Regime Guide — FY 2025-26
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: Finance Act 2024, Section 115BAC
**Target agent**: tax-strategist

## New Regime is Default

From FY 2024-25 onwards, the new tax regime is the DEFAULT. Taxpayers must explicitly opt out to use the old regime. For salaried individuals, the choice can be made each year. For business income, once old regime is chosen, can switch back only once.

## New Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26)

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 4,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs 4,00,001 — 8,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 8,00,001 — 12,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs 12,00,001 — 16,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs 16,00,001 — 20,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs 20,00,000 | 30% |

**Standard deduction**: Rs 75,000 (salaried / pensioners)
**Rebate u/s 87A**: Full tax rebate if taxable income ≤ Rs 12,00,000 (effectively zero tax up to Rs 12.75L for salaried)

## What You Lose in New Regime

Cannot claim:
- Section 80C (PPF, ELSS, insurance, etc.)
- Section 80D (health insurance) — except employer-paid
- HRA exemption
- Section 80CCD(1B) (NPS self-contribution extra 50K)
- Section 24(b) (home loan interest on self-occupied)
- LTA (Leave Travel Allowance)
- Standard deduction on rental income (30%)

## What You Keep in New Regime

Can still claim:
- Standard deduction Rs 75,000 (salaried)
- Section 80CCD(2) — employer NPS contribution (up to 14%/10% of basic)
- Section 80JJAA — new employee deduction
- Gratuity / leave encashment exemptions
- Exemption on VRS (up to Rs 5L)
- Agricultural income exemption

## Strategy for New Regime

Since deductions don't help, focus on:
1. **Employer NPS** (80CCD(2)): Still works — maximize employer contribution
2. **Capital gains management**: Harvest Rs 1.25L LTCG annually (separate from income tax)
3. **HUF**: Create HUF for additional tax-free slab (separate entity)
4. **Tax-free instruments**: PPF maturity (tax-free), SGB maturity (LTCG exempt), insurance maturity (10D exempt)
5. **Salary restructuring**: Less relevant now, but can optimize employer NPS contribution

## Who Should Stay in Old Regime?

Old regime is better ONLY if you have very high deductions:
- Large home loan (>Rs 2L interest) + Section 80C maxed + 80D + NPS + HRA
- Typically need Rs 4L+ in deductions to justify old regime
- Most common for: salaried with home loan in metro city + family health insurance + NPS
