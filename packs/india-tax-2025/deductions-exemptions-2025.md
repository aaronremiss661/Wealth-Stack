# Deductions & Exemptions — FY 2025-26
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: Income Tax Act, 1961 (as amended by Finance Act 2024)
**Target agent**: tax-strategist

## Important: Old Regime Only

Most deductions below are available ONLY under the old tax regime. New regime allows only:
- Standard deduction: Rs 75,000 (increased from 50K in Budget 2024)
- Section 80CCD(2): Employer NPS contribution
- Section 80JJAA: New employment deduction

## Section 80C — Rs 1,50,000 Limit

Eligible investments (aggregate limit):
| Investment | Lock-in | Returns (approx) |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| EPF | Till retirement | 8.25% |
| PPF | 15 years | 7.1% |
| ELSS MF | 3 years | Market-linked |
| NSC | 5 years | 7.7% |
| Tax-saver FD | 5 years | 6.5-7.5% |
| SCSS | 5 years | 8.2% |
| Sukanya Samriddhi | Till girl turns 21 | 8.2% |
| Life insurance premium | Policy term | Varies |
| Tuition fees (2 children) | N/A | N/A |
| Home loan principal | N/A | N/A |

**Ranking by efficiency**: ELSS (3yr lock, market returns) > EPF (employer match) > PPF (tax-free returns) > NPS 80CCD(1B) > NSC/FD

## Section 80D — Health Insurance

| Category | Premium Limit | Preventive Health |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Self/family (below 60) | Rs 25,000 | Included |
| Self/family (60+) | Rs 50,000 | Included |
| Parents (below 60) | Rs 25,000 | Included |
| Parents (60+) | Rs 50,000 | Included |
| **Maximum total** | **Rs 1,00,000** | |

- No insurance but 60+ age: Rs 50,000 deduction for medical expenses
- Payment must be non-cash (digital/cheque)

## Section 80CCD — NPS

| Sub-section | Limit | Regime |
|------------|-------|--------|
| 80CCD(1) | Within 80C's 1.5L | Old only |
| 80CCD(1B) | Additional Rs 50,000 | Old only |
| 80CCD(2) — Employer | 14% of basic (govt) / 10% (private) | Both regimes |

**NPS total tax benefit**: Up to Rs 2,00,000 in old regime (1.5L under 80C + 50K under 80CCD(1B))

## Section 80E — Education Loan Interest

- No upper limit on deduction
- Interest portion only (not principal)
- Available for 8 years from start of repayment
- Loan must be from recognized institution

## Section 80EEA / 80EEB — Removed

- Home loan interest extra deduction (80EEA) and EV loan (80EEB) expired, not available FY25-26

## Section 24(b) — Home Loan Interest

| Property | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Self-occupied | Rs 2,00,000 per year |
| Let out | No limit (actual interest) |
| Under construction | Deduction in 5 installments after possession |

## Section 80G — Donations

| Donation Type | Deduction |
|--------------|-----------|
| PM's National Relief Fund | 100% without limit |
| Approved charitable trusts | 50% with limit (10% of adjusted total income) |
| Political parties (registered) | 100% without limit |

## Section 80TTA / 80TTB — Savings Interest

| Section | For | Limit |
|---------|-----|-------|
| 80TTA | Below 60 | Rs 10,000 on savings interest |
| 80TTB | 60+ | Rs 50,000 on all deposit interest |

## Break-Even: Old vs New Regime

New regime is better if your total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + 80CCD + 24b) are less than:
- Income 10L: ~Rs 3.75L in deductions → Old is better only above this
- Income 15L: ~Rs 3.75L
- Income 20L: ~Rs 4.25L
- Income 50L+: Almost always new regime is better (due to surcharge differences)

**Rule of thumb**: If deductions < Rs 3.75L, new regime wins.
