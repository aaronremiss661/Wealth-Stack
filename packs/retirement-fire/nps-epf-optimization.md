# NPS & EPF Optimization for FIRE
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: PFRDA, EPFO regulations
**Target agent**: retirement-specialist

## NPS (National Pension System)

### Structure
| Tier | Lock-in | Withdrawal | Tax |
|------|---------|-----------|-----|
| Tier I | Till 60 | 60% lump sum + 40% annuity | Lump sum tax-free, annuity taxable |
| Tier II | None | Anytime | No additional tax benefit (except govt employees) |

### Tax Benefits
| Section | Deduction | Available In |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 80CCD(1) | Up to 10% of salary (within 80C 1.5L) | Old regime |
| 80CCD(1B) | Additional Rs 50,000 | Old regime |
| 80CCD(2) — Employer | 14% of basic (govt) / 10% (private) | Both regimes |

**Total possible NPS benefit**: Rs 2,00,000 (old regime) + employer contribution

### Asset Allocation in NPS
| Fund | Asset Class | Return Expectation |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| E (Equity) | Large cap equity | 12-14% long term |
| C (Corporate bonds) | Corporate debt | 8-9% |
| G (Government securities) | Govt bonds | 7-8% |
| A (Alternative) | REITs, InvITs, AIF | 10-12% |

**Active choice**: Choose your own allocation (max 75% equity till age 50, then reduces)
**Auto choice**: Age-based glide path (aggressive/moderate/conservative)

### Optimization for FIRE
1. **Maximize employer contribution** (80CCD(2)) — this works in both tax regimes
2. **Choose Active Choice with max equity** (75%) while young
3. **Don't over-invest in NPS**: The 40% mandatory annuity is inefficient. NPS should be ONE component, not the entire retirement plan
4. **Partial withdrawal**: After 3 years, can withdraw 25% for specific purposes (house, education, medical, marriage) — max 3 times
5. **Early exit**: If corpus <Rs 5L at 60, can withdraw 100% as lump sum

### NPS vs Equity MF Comparison
| Factor | NPS | Equity MF |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| Tax benefit (input) | Rs 50K extra under 80CCD(1B) | None (except ELSS 80C) |
| Expense ratio | ~0.01-0.09% | 0.1-1.0% (direct) |
| Flexibility | Low (locked till 60) | High (anytime redemption) |
| Tax at exit | Annuity taxed at slab | LTCG 12.5% above 1.25L |
| Choice | Limited (7-8 fund managers) | Wide (1000+ funds) |

**Verdict**: Use NPS for the tax benefit only (max 50K under 80CCD(1B) + employer contribution). Invest the rest in equity MFs for flexibility.

## EPF (Employee Provident Fund)

### Current Rates (FY 2024-25)
- **Interest rate**: 8.25% (tax-free up to Rs 2.5L annual contribution)
- **Employee contribution**: 12% of basic
- **Employer contribution**: 3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS (pension)
- **Total**: 24% of basic goes toward retirement

### Tax Treatment
| Event | Tax |
|-------|-----|
| Annual interest (contribution <2.5L) | Tax-free |
| Annual interest (contribution >2.5L) | Taxable on excess |
| Withdrawal after 5 years | Tax-free |
| Withdrawal before 5 years | Taxed at slab rate + TDS 10% |
| EPS pension | Taxable as salary income |

### EPF Optimization
1. **Don't withdraw early**: Premature withdrawal loses tax-free status + compounding
2. **VPF (Voluntary PF)**: Can contribute beyond 12%, but interest on contribution >2.5L is taxable since 2021
3. **On job change**: Always TRANSFER (don't withdraw). Use UAN portal for online transfer
4. **After FIRE/resignation**: Can continue EPF contributions as voluntary member (rarely done, complex process)
5. **EPS at 58**: Choose commutation vs monthly pension based on life expectancy and alternative investments

### EPF Withdrawal Timeline
| Duration without employer contribution | Status |
|---------------------------------------|--------|
| 0-2 months | Active |
| 2-36 months | Inoperative (still earns interest) |
| 36+ months | Can apply for final settlement |

### FIRE Hack: EPF Bridge
- If retiring at 40, EPF can be withdrawn after 2 months of no employer contribution
- This provides a lump sum (tax-free if >5 years of service)
- Use this to fund Bucket 1 (immediate expenses) while letting equity investments grow

## Pension Calculator

For a simple EPF + NPS pension estimate:
- **EPF at 60**: Current balance × (1 + 8.25%)^years
- **NPS at 60**: Current balance × (1 + expected return)^years
- **Monthly pension from NPS annuity**: 40% of NPS corpus × annuity rate (6-7%) ÷ 12
- **EPS pension**: Based on service years and final salary (max Rs 7,500 pensionable salary currently, unless member of higher wage ceiling)
