## Direct vs Regular Plan Impact

The direct-regular delta compounds significantly over time:

| Investment Period | 1% Annual Delta Impact (on Rs 1 Cr) |
|------------------|-------------------------------------|
| 5 years | ~Rs 6.5 lakh difference |
| 10 years | ~Rs 17 lakh difference |
| 20 years | ~Rs 55 lakh difference |
| 30 years | ~Rs 1.3 crore difference |

Assuming 12% gross return, 1% expense difference = 15-20% lower corpus over 20 years.

## SEBI Expense Ratio Caps (TER Slabs)

SEBI mandates maximum TER based on AUM of the scheme:

| AUM Slab (Equity Schemes) | Max TER |
|---------------------------|---------|
| First Rs 500 crore | 2.25% |
| Next Rs 250 crore | 2.00% |
| Next Rs 1,250 crore | 1.75% |
| Next Rs 3,000 crore | 1.60% |
| Next Rs 5,000 crore | 1.50% |
| Next Rs 40,000 crore | TER reduces by 0.05% for every Rs 5,000 Cr |
| Above Rs 50,000 crore | 1.05% |

For debt/hybrid schemes: caps are 0.25% lower at each slab.
Additional 0.30% allowed if 30%+ AUM from B30 cities.

## Tracking Error for Index Funds

| Acceptable Tracking Error | Rating |
|--------------------------|--------|
| < 0.10% | Excellent |
| 0.10% - 0.25% | Good |
| 0.25% - 0.50% | Average |
| > 0.50% | Poor — investigate |

### Common Causes of Tracking Error
1. Cash holdings (undeployed SIP inflows)
2. Transaction costs on rebalancing
3. Dividend timing mismatch
4. Expense ratio drag
5. Securities lending income (can reduce tracking error)

### Index Fund Selection Criteria
1. Tracking error (primary — lower is better)
2. Tracking difference (annualized underperformance vs index)
3. AUM (larger = better — more efficient rebalancing)
4. Expense ratio (lower is better)
5. AMC reputation and index fund track record

## Fund Selection Framework

| Criteria | Weight | Metric |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Performance (3Y, 5Y rolling) | 30% | Alpha vs benchmark |
| Risk-adjusted return | 25% | Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio |
| Expense ratio | 15% | Lower = better |
| Fund manager tenure | 10% | Stability, >3 years preferred |
| AUM | 10% | Not too small (<100Cr) or too large |
| Consistency | 10% | Rolling return quartile ranking |
