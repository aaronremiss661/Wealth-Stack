# National Pension System (NPS) Rules — FY 2025-26

## NPS Account Types

### Tier I (Mandatory Pension Account)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Lock-in | Till age 60 (with exceptions) |
| Minimum contribution | Rs 1,000/year |
| Tax benefit (80CCD(1)) | Up to 10% of salary (within 80C limit of Rs 1.5L) |
| Tax benefit (80CCD(1B)) | Additional Rs 50,000 (old regime only) |
| Tax benefit (80CCD(2)) | Employer contribution up to 14% (govt) / 10% (pvt) — BOTH regimes |
| Partial withdrawal | After 3 years, up to 25%, for specified reasons (3 times max) |
| Exit before 60 | 20% lump sum (tax-free) + 80% annuity (mandatory) |

### Tier II (Voluntary Savings Account)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Lock-in | None (fully liquid) |
| Minimum contribution | Rs 250/year |
| Tax benefit | None (except govt employees: 80CCD(1) for 3-year lock-in) |
| Withdrawal | Anytime, no restrictions |
| Use case | Liquid savings with NPS fund manager returns |

## NPS Asset Classes

| Scheme | Asset Class | Historical Return (10Y CAGR) |
|--------|-----------|------------------------------|
| Scheme E (Equity) | Equity (Nifty 50 + others) | 12-14% |
| Scheme C (Corporate Bond) | Corporate bonds (AA+ and above) | 8-9% |
| Scheme G (Govt Securities) | Government securities | 8-10% |
| Scheme A (Alternative) | REITs, InvITs, CMBS, MBS | 8-10% |

### Asset Allocation Choices

**Active Choice**: Subscriber decides allocation (max 75% in E up to age 50, reduces 2.5% per year after 50)

| Age | Max Equity (Scheme E) |
|-----|----------------------|
| Up to 50 | 75% |
| 51 | 72.5% |
| 55 | 62.5% |
| 60 | 50% |

**Auto Choice (Life Cycle Fund)**:
| Option | Equity at Age 35 | Equity at Age 55 |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Aggressive (LC75) | 75% | 15% |
| Moderate (LC50) | 50% | 10% |
| Conservative (LC25) | 25% | 5% |

## NPS Withdrawal at Maturity (Age 60)

| Component | Rule | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|------|---------------|
| Lump sum withdrawal | Up to 60% of corpus | Tax-free (Section 10(12A)) |
| Annuity purchase | Minimum 40% of corpus | Annuity income taxable at slab rate |
| Complete withdrawal | If total corpus < Rs 5 lakh | 100% lump sum allowed, tax-free |

### Annuity Options (from IRDAI-approved insurers)
1. Annuity for life
2. Annuity for life with return of purchase price on death
3. Annuity for life with annuity to spouse after death
4. Annuity for life with guaranteed period (5, 10, 15, 20 years)

### EPF vs NPS vs PPF Comparison

| Feature | EPF | NPS | PPF |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|
| Return | 8.25% (FY25) | Market-linked (10-13% equity) | 7.1% |
| Tax on contribution | 80C (Rs 1.5L) | 80CCD (Rs 2L total) | 80C (Rs 1.5L) |
| Tax on growth | Exempt (if PF < Rs 2.5L/yr) | Exempt (on 60% lump sum) | Exempt |
| Tax on withdrawal | Exempt (after 5 years) | 60% exempt, annuity taxable | Exempt |
| Lock-in | Till retirement (partial WD allowed) | Till 60 (partial after 3 yrs) | 15 years |
| Employer match | Yes (12% of basic) | Yes (up to 14%/10%) | No |
| Liquidity | Low (partial for home, medical) | Very low | Low (partial from 7th year) |
| EEE status | Yes (within limits) | Partially (60% EEE) | Yes (full EEE) |
