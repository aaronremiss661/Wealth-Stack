# Debt Management Strategies

## Loan Prioritization Framework

### Debt Avalanche (Mathematically Optimal)
- Pay off highest-interest-rate debt first
- Minimum payments on all other debts
- Saves the most in total interest

### Debt Snowball (Psychologically Effective)
- Pay off smallest balance first
- Quick wins build momentum
- Good for clients with multiple small debts

### Recommended Approach for Indian Context

| Priority | Debt Type | Typical Rate | Strategy |
|----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| 1 (Urgent) | Credit card outstanding | 36-42% | Pay off IMMEDIATELY, switch to EMI conversion if needed |
| 2 (High) | Personal loan | 12-18% | Aggressive prepayment or consolidation via LAP |
| 3 (Medium) | Car loan | 8-12% | Prepay if rate > 10%, else continue |
| 4 (Low) | Home loan | 8-9.5% | Strategic prepayment, but don't over-prioritize |
| 5 (Keep) | Education loan | 8-10% | 80E tax benefit; continue normal EMI |

## Home Loan Prepayment Strategy

### When to Prepay
- Early years (first 5-7 years): Maximum interest component — prepayment saves most
- Rate > 9%: Aggressive prepayment recommended
- Rate < 8.5%: Consider investing surplus instead (if equity returns > 12%)

### EMI vs Tenure Reduction
| Option | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Reduce EMI (keep tenure) | Lower monthly burden, less total interest saved |
| Reduce tenure (keep EMI) | Same monthly burden, significantly more interest saved |
| **Recommendation** | Always reduce tenure (unless cash flow is tight) |

### Prepayment Impact Example (Rs 50L loan, 8.5%, 20 years)

| Prepayment | Total Interest Saved | Years Saved |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Rs 1L/year extra | Rs 7.5L | 3.5 years |
| Rs 2L/year extra | Rs 12.8L | 5.5 years |
| Rs 5L/year extra | Rs 20.5L | 9 years |
| Rs 10L lump sum (Year 5) | Rs 10.2L | 3 years |

### Home Loan Balance Transfer
- Switch from higher-rate lender to lower-rate lender
- Worth it if: Rate differential > 0.50% AND remaining tenure > 10 years
- Costs: Processing fee (0.25-0.50%) + legal charges + valuation
- Break-even: Usually 6-12 months

## Debt Consolidation Options

### LAP (Loan Against Property) for Consolidation
| Multiple Debts | Consolidated LAP |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Personal loan: Rs 10L at 14% | |
| Car loan: Rs 5L at 10% | LAP: Rs 15L at 9.5% |
| Credit card: Rs 2L at 36% | Tenure: 10-15 years |
| **Total EMI: Rs 45,000** | **New EMI: Rs 18,000-22,000** |

### Cautions
- Don't convert unsecured debt to secured (property at risk)
- Longer tenure = more total interest (even at lower rate)
- Address the root cause of debt accumulation

## EMI Affordability Guidelines

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Total EMIs (all loans) | < 40% of take-home pay |
| Home loan EMI | < 30% of take-home pay |
| Non-housing EMIs | < 15% of take-home pay |
| Credit card utilization | < 30% of credit limit |

### FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)
- Banks use FOIR for loan eligibility
- FOIR = Total monthly obligations / Gross monthly income
- Acceptable FOIR: 40-50% (varies by lender and income level)
- HNI/salaried with high income: Up to 60% FOIR may be acceptable

## Good Debt vs Bad Debt

| Good Debt | Bad Debt |
|-----------|----------|
| Home loan (appreciating asset + tax benefit) | Credit card revolving balance |
| Education loan (earning capacity + 80E benefit) | Personal loan for lifestyle |
| Business loan (income-generating) | Car loan (depreciating asset, high rate) |
| LAP for business expansion | Loan for vacation/wedding |
| LAS for investment (if spread positive) | EMI on electronics/gadgets |

## Interest Rate Reset Planning

- Review all floating-rate loans annually
- When RBI cuts rates: Ensure EBLR-linked loans reflect new rate within 3 months
- MCLR-linked loans: Consider balance transfer to EBLR-linked (faster transmission)
- Fixed-rate loans: No benefit from rate cuts; consider switching to floating if rates expected to fall further
