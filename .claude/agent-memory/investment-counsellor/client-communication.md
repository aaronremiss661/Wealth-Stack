# Client Communication Frameworks

## Review Meeting Structure

### Quarterly Review Agenda (60-90 Minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| 1. Check-in | 10 min | Life changes, concerns, goals update |
| 2. Market context | 10 min | What happened, what it means (NOT prediction) |
| 3. Portfolio review | 20 min | Performance vs benchmark, allocation drift, holdings |
| 4. Goal progress | 15 min | Goal tracker — on track, ahead, behind |
| 5. Action items | 10 min | Rebalancing, tax harvesting, new SIPs |
| 6. Q&A | 10 min | Client questions, concerns |
| 7. Next steps | 5 min | Timeline for actions, next review date |

### Annual Review — Additional Items
- Risk profile reassessment
- Insurance coverage adequacy
- Estate planning check (will, nomination updates)
- Tax strategy for upcoming year
- Life stage assessment (approaching retirement? New child?)

## Communication Tone Guidelines

### During Bull Markets
- **Avoid**: "Markets will keep rising", "This is the new normal"
- **Use**: "Returns have been strong; this is a good time to rebalance"
- **Key message**: "Let's harvest some gains and lock in progress toward your goals"

### During Bear Markets / Corrections
- **Avoid**: "Don't worry, markets will recover" (dismissive)
- **Use**: "Here's what your portfolio is designed to handle"
- **Key message**: "Your financial plan accounted for this. Here's the data on past recoveries."
- **Show**: SIP cost averaging benefit; historical recovery timelines

### During Volatility / Uncertainty
- **Proactive**: Reach out BEFORE client calls (within 24-48 hours of major market event)
- **Acknowledge**: "I understand this is concerning"
- **Contextualize**: "Here's where we stand relative to your financial plan"
- **Action**: "Here's what we're doing / not doing, and why"

## Crisis Communication Template

### Email Template (After Major Market Fall >5%)

```
Subject: Your Portfolio Update — [Date]

Dear [Name],

Markets experienced a significant move today [briefly describe event].

Here's what this means for your portfolio:
- Your portfolio is designed for [risk profile] — it can absorb this type of movement
- Your emergency fund and near-term goal funds are in [stable instruments]
- Your equity allocation is meant for [X]-year goals — this is temporary volatility

What we are doing:
- Monitoring your portfolio daily
- [If applicable: Looking for tax-loss harvesting opportunities]
- [If applicable: Considering rebalancing into equity at lower prices]

What you should do:
- Continue your SIPs (this is when they work hardest)
- Avoid making reactive changes
- Call me if you'd like to discuss

I'm available for a call at your convenience.

[Advisor Name]
```

## Risk Tolerance Communication

### Before Investment
- "If your Rs 1 Cr portfolio dropped to Rs 65 lakh temporarily, how would you feel?"
- "Have you experienced a 30% market drop before? What did you do?"
- "How stable is your income? Could you continue investing during a downturn?"

### Risk Profile Questionnaire Design

| Dimension | Questions | Weight |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Risk capacity (objective) | Income stability, net worth, time horizon, dependents | 40% |
| Risk tolerance (emotional) | Comfort with loss, past behavior in downturns | 30% |
| Risk need (required return) | Goal gap analysis, required CAGR to meet goals | 30% |

### Presenting Risk to Clients

| Instead of Saying | Say This |
|------------------|----------|
| "This fund has 18% standard deviation" | "In a bad year, this fund could lose 15-20%" |
| "Your Sharpe ratio is 1.2" | "Your returns are solid relative to the risk taken" |
| "You have a 95% VaR of 8%" | "On 19 out of 20 days, you shouldn't lose more than 8%" |
| "Your portfolio beta is 0.8" | "Your portfolio moves 20% less than the market in either direction" |

## Reporting Best Practices

### What to Include
1. Absolute return (since inception, 1Y, 3Y, 5Y)
2. Return vs benchmark (alpha or underperformance)
3. Goal progress (% funded, on-track indicator)
4. Asset allocation (current vs target, with visual pie chart)
5. Top holdings (stocks/funds with weights)
6. Costs paid (total expense, advisory fee, transaction costs)
7. Tax summary (LTCG, STCG, tax saved through harvesting)

### What NOT to Include
- Daily/weekly return fluctuations (induces trading behavior)
- Peer comparison ("your neighbor's portfolio returned...")
- Predictions or forecasts
- Complex jargon without explanation

## Documentation Requirements (SEBI Compliance)

| Document | When | Retention |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Risk profiling questionnaire | At onboarding + annual review | 5 years minimum |
| Investment Policy Statement | At onboarding | 5 years minimum |
| Suitability rationale | For each recommendation | 5 years minimum |
| Fee disclosure | At onboarding + annually | 5 years minimum |
| Client communication log | Ongoing | 5 years minimum |
| Complaint/grievance record | As received | 5 years minimum |
