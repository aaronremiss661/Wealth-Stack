# HNI / UHNI Thresholds & Wealth Tier Classification

## Wealth Tiers — Indian Context

| Tier | Investable Assets | Common Labels |
|------|------------------|---------------|
| Mass Affluent | Rs 50L - Rs 2Cr | Affluent retail |
| HNI | Rs 2Cr - Rs 5Cr | High Net Worth Individual |
| Super HNI | Rs 5Cr - Rs 25Cr | Emerging UHNI |
| UHNI | Rs 25Cr - Rs 100Cr | Ultra High Net Worth |
| Family Office | Rs 100Cr+ | Single/Multi Family Office |

Note: SEBI uses different thresholds for product eligibility:
- PMS minimum: Rs 50 lakh
- AIF minimum: Rs 1 crore
- Accredited Investor: Net worth Rs 7.5Cr (or income Rs 2Cr/yr, or both Rs 5Cr + Rs 1Cr)

## SEBI Accredited Investor Framework (2021)

| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Net worth (individual) | Rs 7.5 crore |
| Annual income | Rs 2 crore per annum |
| Net worth + income (combined) | Rs 5 crore net worth + Rs 1 crore income |
| For body corporate | Rs 50 crore net worth |

Benefits of accredited investor status:
- Lower minimum AIF ticket: Rs 70 lakh (Cat I/II), Rs 70 lakh (Cat III)
- Flexible investment limits in PMS
- Access to large-value AIFs with relaxed compliance
- Custom terms from fund managers

## Common HNI Wealth Structures

1. **Individual + HUF**: Separate PAN, separate tax deductions, LTCG exemption doubling
2. **Family Trust**: Asset protection, smooth succession, privacy
3. **LLP / Company**: Business income routing, retained earnings, lower tax rate
4. **NRI entity**: If family has overseas members, DTAA optimization
5. **Charitable Trust**: 80G benefits, CSR compliance, social impact
