# Concentrated Stock Position Management Strategies

## When to Worry About Concentration

| Threshold | Status | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Single stock < 10% of net worth | Green | Monitor |
| Single stock 10-25% of net worth | Yellow | Plan diversification |
| Single stock 25-50% of net worth | Orange | Active diversification needed |
| Single stock > 50% of net worth | Red | Urgent diversification |

Common situations causing concentration:
- ESOP vesting (IT professionals, startup founders)
- Inherited stock (family business / promoter shares)
- IPO/M&A proceeds in single stock
- Conviction-based holding that has appreciated significantly

## Diversification Strategies

### 1. Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)
- Sell fixed quantity/value monthly/quarterly
- Reinvest in diversified portfolio (MFs, PMS)
- **Tax optimization**: Sell lots with highest cost basis first
- **Timeline**: Spread over 12-24 months to average exit price
- **Tax impact**: 12.5% LTCG above Rs 1.25L per year; 20% if held < 12 months

### 2. LTCG Harvesting (Annual Reset)
- Sell up to Rs 1.25L LTCG per year (tax-free for individual PAN)
- Immediately reinvest in different instrument or buy back after 30+ days
- For HUF: Additional Rs 1.25L tax-free LTCG
- **Best for**: Slow, tax-efficient unwinding over 5-10 years

### 3. Loan Against Securities (LAS)
- Borrow against concentrated holding (50% LTV for equity)
- Use proceeds to invest in diversified portfolio
- **No capital gains tax** triggered (no sale)
- Interest cost: 9-11% (NBFC), 8.5-10% (bank)
- Risk: Margin call if stock drops >30-40%
- **Best for**: Tax-sensitive clients who want to maintain ownership

### 4. Collar Strategy (Options)
- Buy protective put (downside protection)
- Sell covered call (fund the put premium)
- Creates a price range (collar) around current value
- **Requirements**: Lot size available, liquid options market
- **Works for**: Large-cap stocks with active F&O segment
- Cost: Net zero or small debit (depending on strike selection)

### 5. Exchange Fund (Not common in India)
- Pool concentrated positions with other investors
- Each receives proportional share of diversified portfolio
- Limited availability in India; more common in US
- Some PMS/AIFs offer informal versions

### 6. Gifting / HUF Transfer
- Gift shares to family members in lower tax brackets
- Transfer to HUF (creates separate taxable entity)
- **Clubbing provisions**: Spouse income clubs back; minor child income clubs back
- **No clubbing for**: Adult children, parents, HUF (if not from spouse's gift)
- Gift to HUF: Considered HUF property; income taxed in HUF
- Capital gains on subsequent sale: In HUF's hands at HUF's tax rate

### 7. Charitable Donation (Section 80G)
- Donate shares to eligible charitable institution
- Deduction under 80G for donated value
- No capital gains on donation of listed securities (some conditions apply)
- **Best for**: Philanthropic clients with very large concentrated gains

## Decision Framework

| Client Situation | Recommended Strategy | Priority |
|-----------------|---------------------|----------|
| Startup founder post-IPO (locked) | LAS for immediate liquidity + SWP post-lock-in | High |
| IT professional with vested ESOPs | SWP (monthly sale) + LTCG harvesting | High |
| Inherited promoter shares | LAS + gradual SWP + HUF transfer | Medium |
| Long-term conviction holding | Annual LTCG harvest + rebalance rest | Medium |
| Pre-retirement with concentrated pension stock | Aggressive SWP + shift to income assets | Urgent |

## Tax-Aware Execution

| Holding Period | Tax Rate | Strategy |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| < 12 months | 20% STCG | Delay sale if possible to cross 12 months |
| > 12 months, gain < Rs 1.25L | 0% | Sell and reinvest (free reset) |
| > 12 months, gain > Rs 1.25L | 12.5% LTCG | Spread across financial years |
| Loss position | Harvest loss | Sell, book loss, offset against gains |

**Pro tip**: Stagger sales across March-April to use Rs 1.25L exemption in two financial years (sell some in March, rest in April).
