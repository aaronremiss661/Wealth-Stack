# International Investing from India
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: RBI LRS, SEBI regulations, broker platforms
**Affects**: alternative-investments, nri-advisor

## Routes for International Investment

### 1. LRS (Liberalized Remittance Scheme)
- Limit: $250,000 per financial year per individual
- Can invest in: US stocks, ETFs, bonds, real estate, deposits
- TCS: 20% above Rs 7L remittance (claimable as tax credit)
- Platforms: Vested, INDmoney, HDFC Securities (GlobalInvest), ICICI Direct
- Tax: LTCG at slab rate (not 12.5% — these are foreign assets), STCG at slab rate

### 2. Indian Fund of Funds (Feeder Funds)
- Invest in international indices via Indian MF schemes
- Examples: Motilal Oswal Nasdaq 100 FoF, ICICI Pru US Bluechip, Kotak Nasdaq 100 FoF
- Tax: At slab rate regardless of holding period (post-April 2023 change)
- Expense ratio: 0.5-1.5% (on top of underlying fund's cost)
- No LRS limit — invest any amount in INR
- SEBI had temporarily paused new investments in international funds (2022-2023), since resumed with limits

### 3. ETFs on Indian Exchanges
- Motilal Oswal Nasdaq 100 ETF, Mirae Asset NYSE FANG+ ETF, Nippon India Hang Seng ETF
- Tax: Same as equity MF if listed on Indian exchange (LTCG 12.5% >12mo, STCG 20%)
- More tax-efficient than FoFs
- Liquidity can be limited (low trading volumes on some ETFs)

## Recommended International Allocation
- 10-20% of equity portfolio for diversification
- Currency benefit: INR has depreciated ~3-4% p.a. vs USD long-term
- Preferred route: Indian-listed international ETFs (tax-efficient) or LRS for larger allocations
