## Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) — Detailed

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Issuer | Reserve Bank of India (on behalf of Government) |
| Interest | 2.5% p.a. on issue price (paid semi-annually) |
| Interest taxation | Taxable at slab rate |
| Tenure | 8 years, exit option after 5th year (on interest payment dates) |
| LTCG at maturity | Completely tax-exempt (Section 47(viia)) |
| LTCG if sold on exchange before maturity | 12.5% (>12 months holding) |
| Max holding | 4 KG per individual per FY (8 KG for HUF/trust) |
| Tradeable | Yes, on NSE/BSE (but low liquidity) |
| Collateral | Can be used as collateral for loans |

### SGB Issuance Status
- No new issuances since February 2024
- Secondary market only — trading on exchanges
- Premium on secondary market: 5-15% above gold price (reflecting tax benefit)
- Different series available with different maturity dates

### SGB vs Gold ETF Decision

| Situation | Choose SGB | Choose Gold ETF |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Long-term (5-8 years) | Yes (tax-free + interest) | No |
| Need liquidity | No (low secondary liquidity) | Yes |
| Regular income | Yes (2.5% semi-annual) | No (no income) |
| Tax optimization | Yes (zero LTCG at maturity) | Lower priority |
| SIP in gold | No (not available for SIP) | Yes (via Gold MF FoF) |
| NRI investor | Not available | Available |

## Gold in Portfolio Context

### Role of Gold
1. **Portfolio hedge**: Low/negative correlation with equity (-0.1 to -0.15)
2. **Currency hedge**: Gold in INR benefits from INR depreciation
3. **Inflation hedge**: Long-term real return of gold ≈ 1-2% above inflation
4. **Crisis hedge**: Outperforms during market panics and geopolitical stress
5. **Central bank demand**: RBI and global central banks increasing gold reserves

### Recommended Allocation
- Conservative: 10-15% of portfolio
- Moderate: 5-10%
- Aggressive: 3-5%
- Minimum for any diversified portfolio: 5%
