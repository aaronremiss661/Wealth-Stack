## REIT/InvIT Key Rules

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Minimum lot size | 1 unit (reduced from earlier higher lots) |
| Listing | NSE/BSE, traded like stocks |
| Distribution | Mandatory 90% of net distributable cash flow |
| Leverage cap | 49% of total assets (can go to 49% with credit rating) |
| Asset requirement | Minimum 80% in completed, revenue-generating assets |
| Manager fee | Typically 0.5-1.0% of AUM |
| Sponsor holding | Minimum 15% (3-year lock-in) |

## Taxation of REIT/InvIT Distributions

| Component | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|--------------|
| Interest income (from SPV debt) | Taxable at slab rate (TDS 10%) |
| Dividend income (from SPV profits) | Tax-free in hands of unit holder (taxed at SPV level) |
| Return of capital / amortization | Reduces cost of acquisition (no immediate tax) |
| Rental income (REIT) | Taxable at slab rate |

### Capital Gains on REIT/InvIT Units

## Why Include REITs/InvITs in Portfolio

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Regular income | 6-10% yield, quarterly distributions |
| Real estate exposure | Without direct property ownership hassles |
| Inflation hedge | Rental escalations of 5-15% built into leases |
| Liquidity | Traded on exchange (unlike physical real estate) |
| Diversification | Low correlation with equity markets |
| Professional management | Institutional-grade properties, managed by experts |
| Transparency | SEBI-regulated, quarterly disclosures |

## Recommended Allocation

| Portfolio Type | REIT/InvIT Allocation |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Conservative | 5-10% |
| Moderate | 5-10% |
| Aggressive | 3-5% |
| Income-focused / Retirement | 10-15% |

Caution: REITs/InvITs are interest-rate sensitive. Rising rates reduce valuations; falling rates increase them.
