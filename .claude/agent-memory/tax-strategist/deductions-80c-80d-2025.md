# Deductions under Sections 80C, 80D, and Related — FY 2025-26

## Important: Old Regime Only

Most deductions below are available ONLY under the old tax regime.
The new regime (default from FY 2023-24) allows only: 80CCD(2) employer NPS contribution and standard deduction of Rs 75,000.

---

## Section 80C — Limit: Rs 1,50,000

Eligible investments and expenses:

| Instrument | Lock-in | Return (approx.) | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------------------|-------|
| ELSS Mutual Funds | 3 years | Market-linked (12-15% historical) | Shortest lock-in under 80C |
| PPF | 15 years | 7.1% (Q1 FY26) | EEE — exempt at all stages |
| EPF (employee share) | Till retirement | 8.25% (FY 2024-25) | Auto-deducted from salary |
| NSC | 5 years | 7.7% | Interest reinvested, taxable at maturity |
| SCSS | 5 years | 8.2% | For senior citizens (60+) |
| 5-year Tax-Saver FD | 5 years | 6.5-7.5% | Interest fully taxable |
| Life insurance premium | Policy term | N/A | Max 10% of sum assured |
| Tuition fees (2 children) | N/A | N/A | School/college, not coaching |
| Home loan principal | N/A | N/A | Repayment of principal |
| Stamp duty & registration | N/A | N/A | Year of payment only |
| Sukanya Samriddhi (SSY) | Till girl turns 21 | 8.2% | For girl child, EEE status |

## Section 80CCD — NPS Contributions

| Sub-section | Limit | Regime Availability |
|-------------|-------|-------------------|
| 80CCD(1) | Part of 80C Rs 1.5L limit | Old regime only |
| 80CCD(1B) | Additional Rs 50,000 | Old regime only |
| 80CCD(2) | 14% of salary (govt) / 10% of salary (pvt) | BOTH old and new regime |

- NPS Tier I: mandatory lock-in till 60, 60% lump sum (tax-free), 40% annuity (taxable)
- Total possible deduction via 80C + 80CCD(1B) = Rs 2,00,000 in old regime

## Section 80D — Health Insurance Premiums

| Category | Limit (annual premium) |
|----------|----------------------|
| Self, spouse, children | Rs 25,000 |
| Self (senior citizen 60+) | Rs 50,000 |
| Parents (below 60) | Rs 25,000 |
| Parents (senior citizen 60+) | Rs 50,000 |
| Preventive health check-up | Rs 5,000 (within above limits) |

**Maximum possible 80D deduction**: Rs 1,00,000 (if self is senior + parents are senior)

## Section 80E — Education Loan Interest

- No upper limit on deduction
- Interest on loan for higher education (self, spouse, children)
- Available for 8 years from start of repayment
- Loan must be from financial institution or approved charitable institution

## Section 80EEA — Home Loan Interest (Affordable Housing)

- Expired for new loans post 31 March 2022
- Was additional Rs 1,50,000 deduction on interest for homes < Rs 45L stamp duty value

## Section 80G — Donations

| Donation Type | Deduction |
|--------------|-----------|
| PM Relief Fund, CM Relief Fund | 100% without limit |
| Approved charitable institutions | 50% of donated amount |
| Qualifying limit | 10% of adjusted gross total income |

## Section 80TTA / 80TTB — Savings Interest

| Section | For | Limit |
|---------|-----|-------|
| 80TTA | Below 60 years | Rs 10,000 on savings account interest |
| 80TTB | Senior citizens (60+) | Rs 50,000 on all deposit interest |
