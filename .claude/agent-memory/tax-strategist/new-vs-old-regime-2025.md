# New vs Old Income Tax Regime — FY 2025-26

## New Tax Regime (Default from FY 2023-24, Updated FY 2025-26)

As per Union Budget 2025: revised slabs effective from FY 2025-26.

| Taxable Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|---------------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 4,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs 4,00,001 - Rs 8,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 8,00,001 - Rs 12,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs 12,00,001 - Rs 16,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs 16,00,001 - Rs 20,00,000 | 20% |
| Rs 20,00,001 - Rs 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above Rs 24,00,000 | 30% |

- Standard deduction: Rs 75,000 (salary/pension income)
- Rebate under 87A: Full rebate for income up to Rs 12,00,000 (effectively zero tax up to Rs 12.75L for salaried)
- NPS employer contribution 80CCD(2) allowed

### Deductions NOT Available in New Regime
- 80C (ELSS, PPF, LIC, etc.)
- 80D (health insurance)
- 80CCD(1B) (NPS self-contribution)
- HRA exemption
- LTA exemption
- Home loan interest (Section 24b) for self-occupied property
- Professional tax deduction
- 80TTA/80TTB

## Old Tax Regime

| Taxable Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|---------------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs 2,50,001 - Rs 5,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 5,00,001 - Rs 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs 10,00,000 | 30% |

- Senior citizens (60-80): Basic exemption Rs 3,00,000
- Super senior citizens (80+): Basic exemption Rs 5,00,000
- Full access to all deductions: 80C, 80D, 80CCD(1B), HRA, LTA, Sec 24b, etc.
- Standard deduction: Rs 50,000

## Break-Even Analysis

The new regime is better when total deductions are LOW. As a rule of thumb:

| Gross Income | New Regime Better If Deductions < |
|-------------|----------------------------------|
| Rs 10L | ~Rs 2.5L |
| Rs 15L | ~Rs 3.75L |
| Rs 20L | ~Rs 4.25L |
| Rs 30L | ~Rs 4.75L |
| Rs 50L+ | ~Rs 5.5L+ |

**Common scenarios where OLD regime wins:**
- Salaried with HRA in metro city (Rs 3-5L HRA exemption)
- Home loan of Rs 50L+ (up to Rs 2L interest deduction)
- Heavy 80C + 80D + 80CCD(1B) investment (Rs 2.5L+)
- Multiple children's tuition fees

**Common scenarios where NEW regime wins:**
- Self-employed without HRA
- No home loan
- Low deduction usage (<Rs 2-3L)
- Rental income without significant deductions
- Income below Rs 12L (zero tax with rebate)

## Surcharge Rates (Both Regimes)

| Total Income | Old Regime | New Regime |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| Rs 50L - Rs 1Cr | 10% | 10% |
| Rs 1Cr - Rs 2Cr | 15% | 15% |
| Rs 2Cr - Rs 5Cr | 25% | 25% |
| Above Rs 5Cr | 37% | 25% (capped) |

- New regime caps surcharge at 25% (effective max marginal rate ~39%)
- Old regime surcharge can go up to 37% (effective max marginal rate ~42.7%)
- Health & education cess: 4% on tax + surcharge (both regimes)

## HUF Taxation

- HUF is a separate taxable entity with its own PAN
- Can choose old or new regime independently from individual return
- Old regime: HUF gets its own 80C (Rs 1.5L), 80D, etc.
- LTCG exemption: Separate Rs 1.25L for HUF PAN
- Useful for splitting income: gift assets to HUF, income taxed in HUF
- Cannot claim HRA exemption in HUF
