# Portfolio Concentration Limits & Regulatory Norms

## Wealth Advisory Concentration Guidelines

These are prudent advisory limits for client portfolios:

| Parameter | Recommended Limit | Action if Breached |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------|
| Single stock | < 10% of portfolio | FAIL — must diversify |
| Single sector | < 25% of portfolio | FAIL — sector overweight |
| Single bond issuer | < 5% of portfolio | FAIL — credit concentration |
| Single AMC (mutual funds) | < 40% of portfolio | WARN — AMC concentration |
| Illiquid holdings | < 15% of portfolio | FAIL for non-accredited |
| Alternative investments | < 20% of portfolio | WARN for moderate risk |
| Cash/liquid below minimum | > 2% of portfolio | WARN — liquidity buffer |
| Single geography (intl) | < 30% of portfolio | WARN — country risk |

## SEBI Mutual Fund Concentration Rules

| Rule | SEBI Limit |
|------|-----------|
| Large cap fund — large cap stocks | Minimum 80% in top 100 by market cap |
| Mid cap fund — mid cap stocks | Minimum 65% in 101-250 by market cap |
| Small cap fund — small cap stocks | Minimum 65% in 251+ by market cap |
| Flexi cap — equity | Minimum 65% in equity |
| Multi cap — allocation | Minimum 25% each in large/mid/small cap |
| ELSS — equity | Minimum 80% in equity |
| Sector/thematic fund — single sector | No cap (by design), but must disclose |
| Single stock limit (MF scheme) | Max 10% of NAV in single stock |
| Single issuer (debt) | Max 10% of NAV (can go to 12% with board approval) |
| Sector limit (debt) | No explicit cap, but SEBI monitors |

## PMS Concentration Considerations

- PMS has NO regulatory concentration limits (unlike MFs)
- Concentrated PMS strategies may hold 10-20 stocks
- Must disclose strategy style (concentrated vs diversified) to client
- Risk profile must match: concentrated PMS only for aggressive investors
- Common PMS: 15-25 stocks with single stock up to 15-20%

## AIF Concentration Rules

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Cat I (VC) — single investee company | Max 25% of investable funds |
| Cat II — single investee company | Max 25% of investable funds |
| Cat III — single investee company | Max 10% of investable funds |
| Investment in associates | Max 25% of corpus |

## Compliance Check Process

1. Calculate current allocation percentages
2. Compare against risk-profile-specific limits
3. Flag FAIL (hard breach) vs WARN (soft breach)
4. Generate remediation recommendations with specific rebalancing trades
5. Document findings with date stamp for audit trail
6. Schedule next review (quarterly for HNI, semi-annual for retail)
