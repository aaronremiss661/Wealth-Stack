# Goal Planning Frameworks — Indian Context

## Common Financial Goals

### 1. Retirement (Typically Age 60)
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Corpus needed | 25-30x annual expenses (India-adapted safe withdrawal 3-3.5%) |
| Inflation assumption | 6% general, 8% medical |
| Monthly expense estimate | Current lifestyle + medical buffer (30% extra from age 70) |
| Time horizon | 25-35 years of accumulation |
| Post-retirement duration | Plan for 30 years (age 60-90) |
| Asset mix (accumulation) | 60-80% equity (reduces with age via glide path) |
| Asset mix (withdrawal) | 30-40% equity, 40-50% debt, 10-20% cash/gold |

**Example**: Rs 1L/month expenses today, retiring in 20 years at 6% inflation:
- Future monthly expense: Rs 3.21L/month
- Annual expense at retirement: Rs 38.5L
- Corpus needed (28x): ~Rs 10.8 crore

### 2. Child's Higher Education
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Education inflation | 10-12% per annum (India) |
| IIT/NIT (4 years) | Rs 10-15L today → Rs 40-60L in 15 years |
| Private engineering (4 years) | Rs 15-25L today → Rs 60L-1Cr in 15 years |
| MBA India (IIM, 2 years) | Rs 25-30L today → Rs 80L-1Cr in 15 years |
| MS abroad (US, 2 years) | Rs 40-60L today → Rs 1.5-2.5Cr in 15 years |
| MBBS India (private) | Rs 50L-1Cr today → Rs 2-4Cr in 15 years |
| Time horizon | 15-18 years from child's birth |
| Asset mix | 80% equity first 10 years, gradual shift to 50/50 in last 5 years |

### 3. House Purchase
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Property inflation | 7-9% in metros, 5-7% in tier-2 cities |
| Down payment | 20% of property value (mandatory for bank loan) |
| EMI affordability | Max 40% of take-home pay (including all EMIs) |
| Home loan tenure | 15-20 years optimal (not 30 years) |
| Stamp duty + registration | 5-8% of property value (varies by state) |
| Interior + furnishing | 10-15% of property cost |
| Total upfront need | Down payment + stamp duty + furnishing = ~35-40% of property value |

### 4. Wedding
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Wedding inflation | 8-10% per annum |
| Average wedding cost | Rs 15-30L (middle class), Rs 50L-2Cr (upper middle), Rs 2-10Cr (affluent) |
| Time horizon | 10-25 years from child's birth |
| Asset mix | Similar to education — equity heavy early, debt-heavy in last 2-3 years |

### 5. Emergency Fund
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Size | 6-12 months of expenses |
| Salaried (stable job) | 6 months sufficient |
| Self-employed / variable income | 9-12 months |
| Single-income family | 12 months |
| Instruments | Liquid fund (50%), savings account (25%), FD (25%) |
| Rule | Build emergency fund BEFORE starting investment goals |

## Goal Priority Framework

| Priority | Goal | Non-Negotiable? |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Emergency fund | Yes |
| 2 | Health insurance (family) | Yes |
| 3 | Term insurance (if dependents) | Yes |
| 4 | High-interest debt repayment | Yes |
| 5 | Retirement corpus | Yes (start early) |
| 6 | Children's education | Yes (if applicable) |
| 7 | House purchase | Flexible |
| 8 | Lifestyle goals (car, vacation) | Flexible |
| 9 | Wealth building / legacy | Optional |

## Goal Mapping to Investment Products

| Goal Horizon | Recommended Products |
|-------------|---------------------|
| < 1 year | Liquid fund, savings account, ultra-short duration fund |
| 1-3 years | Short duration fund, corporate bond fund, arbitrage fund |
| 3-5 years | Conservative hybrid, balanced advantage, dynamic bond |
| 5-7 years | Aggressive hybrid, flexi cap fund, large cap fund |
| 7-10 years | Flexi cap, mid cap, multi cap |
| 10+ years | Small cap, mid cap, international funds, ELSS |
