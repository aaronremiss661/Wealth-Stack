# NBFC Categories & RBI Regulatory Framework

## NBFC Classification (RBI Scale-Based Regulation, Oct 2022)

### Four-Layer Structure

| Layer | Type | Asset Size | Key Regulations |
|-------|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Base Layer (NBFC-BL) | NBFC-ND (non-deposit, non-systemically important) | < Rs 1,000 Cr | Basic prudential norms |
| Middle Layer (NBFC-ML) | NBFC-ND-SI, deposit-taking NBFCs, HFCs, IFCs | Rs 1,000 Cr+ (or deposit-taking) | Enhanced prudential norms |
| Upper Layer (NBFC-UL) | Top 10 NBFCs by asset size + others identified by RBI | Identified by RBI | Bank-like regulations |
| Top Layer (NBFC-TL) | Ideally empty; NBFCs posing systemic risk | Extreme cases | Most stringent |

### Key NBFC Types

| Type | Full Form | Key Activity |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| NBFC-ICC | Investment and Credit Company | Lending + investments (most common) |
| NBFC-MFI | Micro Finance Institution | Small loans to low-income groups |
| NBFC-Factor | Factoring Company | Purchase of receivables |
| NBFC-IDF | Infrastructure Debt Fund | Long-term infra lending |
| NBFC-IFC | Infrastructure Finance Company | Infra project financing |
| HFC | Housing Finance Company | Home loans (regulated by RBI post-2019) |
| NBFC-P2P | Peer-to-Peer Lending Platform | Marketplace lending |
| NBFC-AA | Account Aggregator | Financial data sharing (no lending) |
| CIC | Core Investment Company | Holding company (90% assets in group investments) |

### Deposit vs Non-Deposit NBFCs

| Feature | NBFC-D (Deposit-taking) | NBFC-ND (Non-deposit) |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Can accept deposits | Yes (with RBI approval) | No |
| Deposit insurance | Not covered by DICGC | N/A |
| Max deposit rate | RBI prescribed (currently 12.5%) | N/A |
| Regulatory intensity | Higher | Lower (unless systemically important) |
| Capital adequacy | 15% CRAR | 15% CRAR |
| NPA norms | 90 days overdue | 90 days overdue |

## Key NBFC Regulatory Norms

### Capital Requirements
| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Minimum NOF (Net Owned Fund) | Rs 10 crore (for new registrations) |
| CRAR (Capital to Risk-weighted Assets) | Minimum 15% |
| Tier I capital | Minimum 10% |
| Leverage ratio (Upper Layer) | Maximum 4x |

### Asset Classification & Provisioning

| Category | Days Overdue | Provision |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Standard | 0-89 days | 0.40% (NBFC-ML/UL) |
| Sub-standard | 90-365 days | 10% |
| Doubtful (1 year) | 366-730 days | 20% |
| Doubtful (2 years) | 731-1095 days | 30% |
| Doubtful (3+ years) | > 1095 days | 50% |
| Loss | Identified as loss | 100% |

### RBI Regulatory Limits for NBFCs
| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Single borrower exposure | 20% of owned funds (25% for infra) |
| Group borrower exposure | 25% of owned funds (35% for infra) |
| Investment in single company shares | 15% of owned funds |
| Total equity investments | 30% of owned funds |
| Concentration in any sector | Internal limits (board approved) |
