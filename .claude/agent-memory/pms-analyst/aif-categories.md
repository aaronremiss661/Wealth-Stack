# AIF Category Differences — Detailed Guide

## Category Comparison

| Feature | Cat I | Cat II | Cat III |
|---------|-------|--------|---------|
| Purpose | Social/economic benefit | PE, debt, residual | Trading, leverage, hedging |
| Leverage | Not allowed | Not allowed | Allowed |
| Govt incentives | Yes (tax pass-through, concessions) | None specific | None |
| Short selling | Not allowed | Not allowed | Allowed |
| Fund life | Minimum 3 years | Minimum 3 years | Open or closed ended |
| Extension | Up to 2 years | Up to 2 years | N/A for open-ended |
| Min investment | Rs 1 crore | Rs 1 crore | Rs 1 crore |
| Min corpus | Rs 20 crore | Rs 20 crore | Rs 20 crore |
| Max investors | 1,000 per scheme | 1,000 per scheme | 1,000 per scheme |
| Manager commitment | 2.5% or Rs 5Cr (lower) | 2.5% or Rs 5Cr (lower) | 5% or Rs 10Cr (lower) |

## Category I Sub-Categories

### Venture Capital Funds (VCF)
- Invest in early-stage startups (Series A/B)
- Minimum 66.67% in unlisted equity of VC undertakings
- Cannot invest in companies with net tangible assets > Rs 20Cr (at investment time)
- Angel fund: Sub-category, min Rs 25L per investor, max Rs 10Cr per scheme
- Typical return expectation: 20-30% IRR (high risk, high return)

### SME Funds
- Invest in listed/unlisted SMEs
- SEBI definition: Turnover < Rs 250Cr
- Promote MSME sector growth
- Government co-investment schemes available

### Social Venture Funds
- Impact investing focus
- At least 75% of investable funds in social ventures
- Social enterprises serving bottom of pyramid
- Blended finance structures (grant + equity)

### Infrastructure Funds
- Invest in infrastructure companies/projects
- Roads, ports, power, telecom, urban infra
- Government viability gap funding (VGF) eligibility
- Longer gestation, predictable cash flows

## Category II Sub-Categories

### Private Equity Funds
- Growth equity and buyout strategies
- Invest in mid-to-large private companies
- Typical fund life: 7-10 years (3-5 years investment period + 4-5 years harvest)
- J-curve effect: Negative returns initially, positive later
- Indian PE: Healthcare, fintech, SaaS, consumer are hot sectors

### Debt Funds
- Structured credit, mezzanine, high-yield
- Invest in performing and stressed credit
- Returns: 12-16% targeted
- Security: Typically secured by assets/pledges
- Popular for NBFC lending, real estate credit

### Fund of Funds
- Invest in other AIFs or MFs
- Diversification across strategies/managers
- Additional layer of fees (fund-level + underlying fund)
- Useful for first-time AIF investors

### Distressed Asset Funds
- Invest in stressed/NPA assets
- IBC (Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code) resolution
- NARCL and other bad bank structures
- High return potential (30-50%+) but very illiquid and risky

## Category III Sub-Categories

### Long-Short Equity (Hedge Funds)
- Use leverage and short-selling
- Market-neutral or directional strategies
- Derivatives-heavy portfolio
- Return target: 15-20% with lower correlation to market

### PIPE (Private Investment in Public Equity)
- Invest in listed companies via preferential allotment
- Buy at discount to market price
- Lock-in period: 6 months (SEBI)
- Quick deployment, moderate return expectation

## Taxation Summary

| Aspect | Cat I & II | Cat III |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Tax level | Pass-through (investor level) | Fund level (maximum marginal rate) |
| Equity LTCG | 12.5% (in investor hands) | 12.5% (at fund level, 42.7% effective) |
| Equity STCG | 20% (in investor hands) | 20% (at fund level) |
| Dividend | Slab rate (investor) | Maximum marginal rate (fund) |
| Business income | Not pass-through (taxed at fund) | Fund level |
| Interest income | Pass-through | Fund level |

**Key implication**: Cat III is tax-inefficient because all income is taxed at maximum marginal rate (~42.7%) regardless of individual investor's actual tax slab. Cat I/II are more tax-efficient as income flows through to investor's own slab.

## AIF Due Diligence Checklist

1. SEBI registration verification
2. Fund manager track record (prior funds, realized IRR)
3. Investment thesis clarity
4. Fee structure and alignment of interest
5. Portfolio construction rules (concentration, sector limits)
6. Governance (advisory board, IC composition)
7. Auditor and custodian quality
8. Distribution waterfall understanding
9. Clawback provisions
10. Key-person clause (what happens if fund manager leaves)
