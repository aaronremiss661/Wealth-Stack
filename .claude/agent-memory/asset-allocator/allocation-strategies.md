# Asset Allocation Strategies

## Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

Long-term target allocation based on risk profile, time horizon, and financial goals.

### Core Principles
1. Asset allocation drives 85-90% of portfolio return variability (Brinson study)
2. SAA should be reviewed annually, not changed based on market conditions
3. Based on expected returns, volatility, and correlations of asset classes

## Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA)

Short-term deviations from SAA based on market conditions.

### TAA Signals

| Signal | Indicator | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Equity overvalued | Nifty PE > 24, PB > 4.0 | Reduce equity 5-10% |
| Equity undervalued | Nifty PE < 18, PB < 2.8 | Increase equity 5-10% |
| Rising interest rates | RBI hiking cycle | Reduce duration, increase short-term debt |
| Falling interest rates | RBI cutting cycle | Increase duration (gilt, long-term debt) |
| High VIX (>20) | Market fear | Opportunity to increase equity (contrarian) |
| Low VIX (<12) | Complacency | Consider hedging / reducing equity |
| INR weakening | USD/INR rising | Increase gold, international allocation |
| Strong FII inflows | Sustained buying | Momentum supportive of equity |

### TAA Constraints
- Maximum deviation from SAA: +/- 10% per asset class
- No more than 2 tactical shifts per quarter
- Revert to SAA if conviction dissipates within 6 months
- Document every TAA decision with rationale

## Glide Path — Life Stage Allocation

### Age-Based Glide Path (Equity Allocation)

| Age | Aggressive Glide | Moderate Glide | Conservative Glide |
|-----|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 25 | 90% | 80% | 70% |
| 30 | 85% | 75% | 60% |
| 35 | 80% | 65% | 50% |
| 40 | 70% | 55% | 40% |
| 45 | 60% | 45% | 35% |
| 50 | 50% | 35% | 25% |
| 55 | 40% | 30% | 20% |
| 60 | 30% | 25% | 15% |
| 65+ | 25% | 20% | 15% |

### "To" vs "Through" Glide Path
- **"To" retirement**: Equity hits lowest at retirement age (NPS uses this)
- **"Through" retirement**: Equity continues declining past retirement age
- For India (30-year retirement period): "Through" is safer

## Core-Satellite Approach

| Component | Allocation | Purpose | Instruments |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Core (70-80%) | Stable, diversified | Market return + low cost | Index funds, large cap, balanced |
| Satellite (20-30%) | Alpha-seeking | Outperformance | Active mid/small cap, sectoral, PMS |

Benefits:
- Core provides stability and low cost (0.1-0.3% expense ratio)
- Satellite allows targeted bets without risking entire portfolio
- Easy to evaluate alpha from satellite vs benchmark
