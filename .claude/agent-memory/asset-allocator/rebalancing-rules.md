# Rebalancing Rules & Methodology

## Rebalancing Methods

### 1. Calendar-Based Rebalancing
| Frequency | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| Annual | Simple, low transaction cost | May miss large drifts | Most retail investors |
| Semi-annual | Good balance | Moderate effort | HNI investors |
| Quarterly | Responsive | Higher cost, potential over-trading | Institutional, large portfolios |

### 2. Threshold-Based Rebalancing
| Threshold | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| +/- 5% absolute drift | Rebalance that asset class to target |
| +/- 20% relative drift | Rebalance (e.g., 60% target drifts to 72% = 20% relative) |

Example: If target equity is 60% and it drifts to 66%+, sell equity and buy debt to restore 60%.

### 3. Hybrid Method (Recommended)
- Calendar check: Every 6 months
- Threshold override: If any asset class drifts >5 absolute percentage points, rebalance immediately
- Combines discipline with responsiveness

## Rebalancing Decision Matrix

| Current State | Action | Urgency |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| Equity overweight by <3% | Monitor, no action | Low |
| Equity overweight by 3-5% | Rebalance at next calendar date | Medium |
| Equity overweight by >5% | Rebalance immediately | High |
| Equity underweight by >5% (market crash) | Deploy from debt to equity | High (opportunity) |
| Gold overweight by >3% | Trim gold, reallocate | Medium |
| Cash above 10% of portfolio | Deploy into target allocation | Medium |

## Tax-Efficient Rebalancing Strategies

### Strategy 1: Cash Flow Rebalancing
- Direct new SIP investments into underweight asset class
- Direct dividends/interest into underweight category
- Zero capital gains tax impact
- **Best for**: Ongoing SIP investors

### Strategy 2: Tax-Loss Harvesting + Rebalancing
- Sell positions with losses to rebalance (book loss for tax offset)
- Simultaneously buy replacement fund in same category
- Avoid wash sale (buy different fund, not same)
- **Best for**: Bear market rebalancing

### Strategy 3: LTCG Exemption Usage
- Sell equity up to Rs 1.25L LTCG per year (tax-free)
- Use proceeds to buy underweight asset class
- Spread rebalancing across March-April for two FY exemptions
- **Best for**: Long-held equity positions

### Strategy 4: New Money Deployment
- If receiving bonus, matured FD, or other lump sum
- Invest entirely in underweight asset class
- No selling required
- **Best for**: Lump sum events

## Transaction Cost Considerations

| Cost | Rate | Impact on Rebalancing |
|------|------|---------------------|
| STT (equity delivery) | 0.1% | Minor for equity MF (0.001% on redemption) |
| Exit load (MF) | 0-1% (if < 1 year) | Avoid rebalancing MFs held < 1 year |
| Stamp duty | 0.005% (MF purchase) | Negligible |
| STCG tax | 20% on equity gains | Major — avoid selling equity held < 12 months |
| LTCG tax | 12.5% above Rs 1.25L | Moderate — plan for annual exemption |
| Spread (bond funds) | NAV-based, none | No impact |

## Rebalancing Triggers for Major Events

| Life Event | Rebalancing Action |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Job loss | Increase cash bucket, reduce equity |
| Marriage | Review risk profile, possibly reduce risk |
| Child birth | Start child education goal, adjust allocation |
| Home purchase (large EMI) | Reduce equity exposure (lower risk capacity) |
| Inheritance / windfall | Deploy per target allocation (not all in equity) |
| 5 years from goal | Begin shifting from equity to debt (glide path) |
| 2 years from goal | Shift 80%+ of goal corpus to short-term debt |
| Retirement | Execute bucket strategy, major rebalancing |

## Rebalancing Policy Statement (Template)

```
Target Allocation: Equity [X]%, Debt [Y]%, Gold [Z]%
Rebalancing method: Hybrid (semi-annual + 5% threshold)
Calendar dates: January and July
New money: Always directed to underweight class
Tax approach: Use LTCG exemption; avoid STCG
Review: Annual review of target allocation itself
Exceptions: Major market events (>15% fall or rise in 30 days)
```
