# Startup Valuation & Angel Tax

Source: Income Tax Act Section 56(2)(viib), DPIIT notifications, Rule 11UA

## Section 56(2)(viib) — Angel Tax

### Major Update — Budget 2024
**Section 56(2)(viib) has been ABOLISHED for ALL investors effective FY 2024-25.**

| Period | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Before FY 2024-25 | Angel tax applied to resident investors; exemptions for DPIIT-recognized startups |
| FY 2024-25 onwards | Section 56(2)(viib) REMOVED entirely — no angel tax for any investor |

This means startups can raise funds at any valuation without angel tax concern.

### Historical Context
- Tax was on excess of issue price over FMV
- Rate: Company's applicable slab rate (25-30%)
- Exemptions existed for: DPIIT startups, investments from VCs, AIFs, listed entities
- FMV had to be determined by registered merchant banker (DCF method)
- Caused significant friction in startup fundraising; many assessment notices issued

## Valuation Methods (Rule 11UA)

| Method | When to Use | Formula | Best For |
|--------|-------------|---------|----------|
| DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) | Startups with projections | PV of projected free cash flows | Revenue/growth-stage startups |
| NAV (Net Asset Value) | Asset-heavy companies | (Assets - Liabilities) / Shares | Real estate, manufacturing |
| Market approach (comparables) | When peer data available | Revenue/EBITDA/PE multiple of peers | SaaS, fintech |
| Recent transaction | Post-funding round | Price from last arm's-length round | Any stage (most defensible) |
| Berkus Method | Pre-revenue | $500K per factor (idea, prototype, team, partnerships, sales) | Angel/pre-seed |
| Scorecard Method | Pre-revenue | Adjust average pre-money by weighted factors | Angel stage |
| Risk Factor Summation | Early stage | Adjust base valuation by 12 risk factors | Seed stage |

**Who can value**: Registered merchant banker (Category I) for DCF/NAV per Rule 11UA.

## DPIIT Startup Recognition

| Criteria | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Age | Incorporated < 10 years ago |
| Turnover | < Rs 100 Cr in any FY since incorporation |
| Innovation | Working towards innovation, development, or improvement |
| Not a restructure | Not formed by splitting/reconstruction of existing business |
| Registration | Apply on startupindia.gov.in |

### Benefits of DPIIT Recognition
1. Tax holiday: 80-IAC — 3 consecutive years of exemption out of first 10 years
2. Self-certification for labour and environment laws
3. Fast-tracked patent applications (80% rebate on patent fees)
4. Easy winding up (within 90 days under IBC)
5. Fund of Funds access (SIDBI)
6. Government procurement relaxation (no prior experience needed)
7. ESOP tax deferral for employees

## 409A-Equivalent Valuation in India

While India doesn't have a direct equivalent of US IRC Section 409A, similar principles apply:

| US 409A Concept | India Equivalent |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Independent valuation | Merchant banker valuation (Rule 11UA) |
| Safe harbor methods | DCF, NAV methods per Income Tax Rules |
| Frequency | At least annually, or on significant events (funding, M&A) |
| Purpose | Defend exercise price for tax purposes | Determine FMV for perquisite calculation |
| Penalty for non-compliance | Additional tax + interest | Assessment proceedings, demand notice |

## Liquidation Preferences

| Type | Meaning | Impact on Founders |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| 1x Non-participating | Investor gets back investment OR shares pro-rata (not both) | Standard, founder-friendly |
| 1x Participating | Investor gets back investment AND shares pro-rata | More investor-friendly |
| 2x+ Non-participating | Investor gets 2x investment OR shares pro-rata | Aggressive, high-protection |
| Participating with cap | Participation capped at a multiple | Balanced compromise |

**India norm**: Most early-stage deals have 1x non-participating liquidation preference.

### Impact of Liquidation Preference — Example
Company sold for Rs 50 Cr. Investor put in Rs 10 Cr for 20% stake.

| Scenario | 1x Non-Participating | 1x Participating |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Investor gets | Max of (Rs 10 Cr, 20% of Rs 50 Cr = Rs 10 Cr) = Rs 10 Cr | Rs 10 Cr + 20% of (50-10) = Rs 18 Cr |
| Founders/others get | Rs 40 Cr | Rs 32 Cr |

## Startup Investment Due Diligence for Angel/HNI Investors

| Area | Key Checks |
|------|-----------|
| Legal | Company incorporation docs, SHA, articles, IP assignments |
| Financial | Audited financials, cash burn, unit economics, cap table |
| Tax | Angel tax compliance (historical), ESOP scheme, GST registration |
| Regulatory | DPIIT recognition, sector-specific licenses |
| Team | Founder background, previous exits, full-time commitment |
| Market | TAM/SAM/SOM analysis, competitive landscape |
| Product | PMF signals, retention metrics, technology stack |
| Terms | Valuation, liquidation preference, anti-dilution, board seats |
