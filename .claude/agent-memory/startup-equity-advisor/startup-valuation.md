# Startup Valuation & Angel Tax
**Last updated**: 2025-03-25
**Source**: Income Tax Act Section 56(2)(viib), DPIIT notifications
**Affects**: startup-equity-advisor, early-stage-investor

## Section 56(2)(viib) — Angel Tax
- Applies when: Unlisted company issues shares at premium to certain investors
- Tax trigger: If issue price > FMV, the excess is taxed as "Income from Other Sources" in the company's hands
- Rate: At the company's applicable slab rate
- Exemptions (effective from 2024): DPIIT-recognized startups with turnover <Rs 100Cr are **exempt**
- Also exempt: Shares issued to VCs, registered AIFs, and certain listed entities
- Category I AIF investments: Fully exempt regardless of valuation

## Valuation Methods (Rule 11UA)
| Method | When to Use | Formula |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) | Most common for startups | Present value of projected free cash flows |
| NAV (Net Asset Value) | Asset-heavy companies | (Assets - Liabilities) / Shares |
| Market approach | Comparable listed companies | Revenue/EBITDA/PE multiple of peers |
| Recent transaction | Post-funding round | Price from last round with arm's length investor |

**Who can value**: Registered merchant banker (Category I) for DCF/NAV per Rule 11UA.

## DPIIT Startup Recognition
| Criteria | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Age | Incorporated < 10 years ago |
| Turnover | < Rs 100 Cr in any FY since incorporation |
| Innovation | Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/services |
| Not a restructure | Not formed by splitting/reconstruction of existing business |
| Registration | Apply on startupindia.gov.in |

### Benefits of DPIIT Recognition
1. Angel tax exemption (Section 56(2)(viib))
2. Tax holiday: 80-IAC — 3 consecutive years of tax exemption out of first 10 years
3. Self-certification for labour and environment laws
4. Fast-tracked patent applications (80% rebate on patent fees)
5. Easy winding up (within 90 days under Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code)
6. Fund of Funds access (SIDBI)

## Liquidation Preferences (For Angel Investors)
| Type | Meaning | Common In |
|------|---------|-----------|
| 1x Non-participating | Get investment back OR share in upside (not both) | India (standard) |
| 1x Participating | Get investment back AND share in remaining pro-rata | Less common, more investor-friendly |
| 2x+ Liquidation pref | Get 2x investment before anyone else | Later-stage, aggressive terms |

**India norm**: Most early-stage Indian deals have 1x non-participating liquidation preference.
