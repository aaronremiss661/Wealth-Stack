# Family Office Structures — India

## Thresholds & Types

| Type | AUM Threshold | Staff | Services |
|------|-------------|-------|----------|
| Virtual Family Office | Rs 25-100 Cr | Outsourced | Advisory, investment mgmt via external partners |
| Single Family Office (SFO) | Rs 100-500 Cr | 3-10 | Dedicated investment, tax, legal, lifestyle |
| Established SFO | Rs 500-2,000 Cr | 10-25 | Full-service, multi-generational |
| Multi Family Office (MFO) | Rs 25 Cr+ per family | 15-50+ | Serves multiple families, shared infrastructure |
| Institutional Family Office | Rs 2,000 Cr+ | 25-100+ | In-house CIO, PE deals, philanthropy, governance |

## Common Legal Structures

### 1. Private Limited Company
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Tax rate | 25% (if turnover < Rs 400Cr) or 22% (new regime, Sec 115BAA) |
| Dividend taxation | DDT abolished; dividend taxed in shareholder hands at slab |
| Advantages | Limited liability, perpetual succession, easier to raise capital |
| Disadvantages | Double taxation (company + dividend), compliance heavy |
| Best for | Active business families, holding company structure |

### 2. LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Tax rate | 30% + surcharge + cess (no concessional rate) |
| Profit distribution | Not taxable in hands of partners (unlike company dividend) |
| Advantages | No DDT equivalent, flexible management, limited liability |
| Disadvantages | Higher tax rate, cannot raise equity capital |
| Best for | Professional service families, advisory businesses |

### 3. Trust Structure
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Private trust (specific) | Income taxed in beneficiary's hands at their slab rate |
| Private trust (discretionary) | Income taxed at maximum marginal rate (42.7%) |
| Advantages | Asset protection, succession planning, privacy |
| Disadvantages | Complex compliance, irrevocable trust restrictions |
| Best for | Inter-generational wealth transfer, asset protection |

### 4. HUF (Hindu Undivided Family)
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Tax rate | Same slabs as individual |
| Advantages | Separate PAN, separate deductions, LTCG exemption |
| Disadvantages | Only for Hindus/Jains/Sikhs/Buddhists, partition risk |
| Best for | First-generation wealth structuring, splitting income |

## Family Office Functions

| Function | In-House vs Outsourced | Priority |
|----------|----------------------|----------|
| Investment management | In-house (CIO) or outsourced (PMS/MFO) | Critical |
| Tax planning & compliance | In-house CA + external tax firm | Critical |
| Legal & estate planning | External law firm + in-house coordinator | Critical |
| Insurance & risk management | External broker + in-house review | High |
| Real estate management | In-house property manager | Medium |
| Philanthropic advisory | Outsourced or part-time | Medium |
| Family governance | External facilitator + family council | High |
| Lifestyle / concierge | Outsourced | Low |
| Reporting & MIS | In-house + technology platform | Critical |
| Next-gen education | Outsourced programs | Medium |

Includes: Staff salaries, office, technology, external advisors, compliance costs.

## Philanthropy Structures

| Structure | Tax Benefit | Compliance |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Section 80G donation | 50% or 100% deduction | Donation to registered charity |
| CSR spending (Sec 135) | Not tax deductible, mandatory for qualifying companies | 2% of average net profits |
| Charitable Trust (Sec 12A) | 85% of income must be spent on charitable objects | IT registration + FCRA (for foreign funds) |
| Section 10(23C) | Full exemption for educational/medical institutions | Specific conditions |
