# Succession Planning — Frameworks & Best Practices

## Family Governance Framework

### Three-Circle Model (Family / Ownership / Business)

| Circle | Key Questions |
|--------|--------------|
| Family | Who are family members? What are their aspirations? |
| Ownership | Who owns what? How are ownership decisions made? |
| Business | Who runs the business? What is the management structure? |

Conflicts arise at circle intersections. A governance framework addresses each intersection.

## Family Constitution (Charter) Components

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Family values & vision | Shared principles guiding wealth management |
| Family council structure | Decision-making body (members, meeting frequency) |
| Employment policy | Rules for family members joining the business |
| Compensation policy | How family members are paid (market-linked, performance) |
| Distribution policy | Dividend/income distribution rules |
| Conflict resolution | Mediation/arbitration mechanism |
| Next-gen development | Education, mentorship, board observer roles |
| Philanthropy charter | Family giving philosophy and structure |
| Exit provisions | How a family member can exit ownership |

## Business Succession Planning

### Succession Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Preparation | 5-10 years | Identify successors, develop competencies |
| Transition | 2-5 years | Shadow roles, gradual handover |
| Completion | 1-2 years | Full transfer of responsibilities |
| Post-transition | Ongoing | Advisory role for outgoing leader, review |

### Successor Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Assessment Method |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Competence | 30% | Education, industry experience, past results |
| Leadership | 25% | Team management, decision-making ability |
| Vision alignment | 20% | Shared family values, strategic vision |
| Commitment | 15% | Willingness to lead, lifestyle compatibility |
| Family acceptance | 10% | Other family members' support |

### Professional Management vs Family Succession

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Family successor | Cultural continuity, loyalty, long-term view | Limited talent pool, entitlement risk |
| Professional CEO | Wider talent, meritocratic | Culture mismatch, agency problems |
| Hybrid (family chair + professional CEO) | Best of both | Potential conflict, role clarity needed |

## Wealth Succession Structures

### Direct Transfer
| Method | Tax Impact | Timing |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Gift during lifetime | No gift tax in India (abolished 1998) | Immediate |
| Will / inheritance | No inheritance tax in India | On death |
| HUF partition | No CGT on partition | Anytime |

Note: While India has no estate/inheritance tax, this can change. Always plan with flexibility.

### Indirect Transfer (Structures)
| Structure | Mechanism | Advantage |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Family trust | Settle assets into trust, beneficiaries receive income/corpus | Asset protection, controlled distribution |
| Holding company | Transfer shares of holding company | Centralized control, easy to transfer |
| Partnership / LLP | Make successors partners | Gradual transfer of ownership |
| Insurance trust | Life insurance in trust | Tax-free death benefit, immediate liquidity |

## Common Succession Mistakes

1. **No written plan**: Verbal promises lead to disputes
2. **Equal vs equitable**: Equal division may not be fair (active vs passive family members)
3. **Ignoring in-laws**: In-laws influence family dynamics significantly
4. **No timeline**: "Someday" never comes — set specific dates
5. **Single point of failure**: All authority with one person
6. **Ignoring tax implications**: Especially for NRI family members (FEMA, DTAA)
7. **No will or outdated will**: Intestate succession creates conflicts
8. **Mixing personal and business assets**: Complicates succession
9. **Not training next generation**: Sudden handover to unprepared successors
10. **Ignoring daughter's rights**: Hindu Succession Act 2005 gives equal rights
