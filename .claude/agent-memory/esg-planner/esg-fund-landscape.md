## Green Bonds

### What Are Green Bonds?
- Debt instruments where proceeds are used for environmental projects
- Renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean transport, sustainable water

### Indian Green Bond Market

| Issuer Type | Examples | Typical Yield |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| Government (Sovereign Green Bonds) | GoI SGrB (issued 2023, 2024) | 7.0-7.3% |
| PSU | IREDA, NTPC, PFC, REC, SBI | 7.5-8.5% |
| Corporate | ReNew Power, Adani Green, Greenko | 8.0-9.5% |
| Bank | SBI, Axis Bank, YES Bank (historical) | 7.5-8.5% |

### Sovereign Green Bond (SGrB)
- First issued: January 2023 (5-year and 10-year)
- Traded on NDS-OM and stock exchanges
- Proceeds for: National Solar Mission, metro projects, clean transport
- Typically trade at 5-10 bps "greenium" (slightly lower yield than regular G-Sec)

### Green Bond Frameworks
- ICMA Green Bond Principles (international standard)
- SEBI disclosure requirements for green debt securities
- Second-party opinion (SPO) required for certification
- Annual use-of-proceeds report mandatory

## ESG Investing Considerations for Indian Portfolios

### Pros
1. Risk mitigation: ESG screens out companies with governance/environmental risks
2. Long-term alpha: High-ESG companies tend to have lower cost of capital
3. Regulatory direction: BRSR, carbon markets — ESG will become more important
4. Alignment with global capital: FIIs increasingly use ESG mandates

### Cons / Challenges
1. Limited universe: India ESG data quality still evolving
2. Sector bias: ESG screens often underweight energy, metals, tobacco
3. Greenwashing risk: Some funds use ESG label without rigorous screening
4. Short track record: Most Indian ESG funds have <5 years of data
5. Rating divergence: Different ESG raters give different scores to same company

### Practical Allocation

| Investor Type | ESG Allocation |
|--------------|---------------|
| ESG-aware (basic) | 10-20% in ESG funds as satellite |
| ESG-focused | 40-60% in ESG-screened instruments |
| Impact-driven | 80-100% ESG + direct impact investments |
