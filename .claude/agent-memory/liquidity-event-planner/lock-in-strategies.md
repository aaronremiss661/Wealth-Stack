# Lock-In Period Strategies

## Common Lock-In Scenarios

### IPO Lock-In Periods (SEBI Regulations)

| Shareholder Category | Lock-In Period | Can Pledge? |
|---------------------|---------------|-------------|
| Promoters (minimum 20% holding) | 18 months from listing | Yes, to scheduled banks after 6 months |
| Promoters (above 20% holding) | 6 months from listing | Yes, to scheduled banks |
| Non-promoter pre-IPO shareholders | 6 months from listing | No (during lock-in) |
| Anchor investors (institutional) | 30 days (50% of allocation), 90 days (remaining 50%) | No |
| ESOP holders (specific) | Per company ESOP plan | Depends on plan |
| Retail IPO allottees | No lock-in | Yes |

### ESOP Lock-In Scenarios

| Scenario | Typical Lock-In | Strategy |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Cliff period | 1 year (no vesting before cliff) | Plan cash flow without ESOP liquidity |
| Vesting schedule (standard) | 4 years (25% per year) | Sell vested portions as they unlock |
| Post-exercise hold | Company may mandate 1-6 months | Use LAS if cash needed during hold |
| Pre-IPO ESOP | No sale until IPO or secondary sale | Explore secondary markets (IndiaCrossingover, Unlistedkart) |

### Investment Product Lock-Ins

| Product | Lock-In | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| ELSS | 3 years | Shortest lock-in under 80C |
| PPF | 15 years (partial after year 7) | EEE tax status |
| NPS Tier I | Till 60 (partial after 3 years) | Tax-efficient retirement |
| Tax-saver FD | 5 years | Lower returns but guaranteed |
| SGB | 5 years (early exit), 8 years (maturity) | Tax-free at maturity |
| AIF Cat I/II | 3 years minimum + 2 yr extension | Closed-ended |

## Strategies During Lock-In Period

### 1. Loan Against Securities (LAS)
- Pledge locked-in shares and borrow against them
- Available for listed IPO shares (even during promoter lock-in, with conditions)
- LTV: 50% for equity
- Interest: 8.5-12%
- **Use case**: Need liquidity during lock-in without selling

### 2. Collar Strategy (For Listed Locked-In Shares)
- Buy protective put at lower strike
- Sell covered call at higher strike
- Creates a price band — limits both upside and downside
- **Limitation**: Only works if F&O available for the stock
- **SEBI rule**: Promoters cannot write call options on their locked-in shares

### 3. Pre-IPO Secondary Sale
- For ESOP/pre-IPO holders wanting liquidity before IPO
- Platforms: IndiaCrossingover, Unlistedkart, Precize (informal markets)
- Discount to expected IPO price: 10-30%
- Company board approval usually required
- Right of First Refusal (ROFR) clause check mandatory

### 4. Systematic Exit Plan (Post Lock-In)

#### Phase 1: Immediate Unlock (Month 1-3)
| Action | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| Sell enough to cover tax liability | Calculate and set aside |
| Sell for emergency fund top-up | 12-18 months expenses |
| Pay off high-interest debt | Credit card, personal loans |

#### Phase 2: Diversification (Month 3-12)
| Action | Approach |
|--------|---------|
| Sell 5-10% per month | Reduce concentration gradually |
| Reinvest in diversified portfolio | STP into equity MF + debt allocation |
| Consider LTCG exemption | Spread sales across financial years |

#### Phase 3: Steady State (Month 12+)
| Action | Approach |
|--------|---------|
| Maintain max 10-15% in single stock | Ongoing SWP from concentrated position |
| Full portfolio constructed | Goal-linked asset allocation |
| Ongoing monitoring | Quarterly review |

## Tax Planning Around Lock-In Expiry

| Strategy | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Split across FY | If lock-in expires near March, sell some before March 31 and rest after April 1 |
| Use Rs 1.25L LTCG exemption | Sell up to Rs 1.25L gains tax-free per year per PAN |
| HUF strategy | Transfer some shares to HUF before sale for additional Rs 1.25L exemption |
| Set off losses | If other holdings are in loss, sell those first to offset gains |
| Advance tax | Pay 15% by Jun 15, 45% by Sep 15, 75% by Dec 15, 100% by Mar 15 |

## Red Flags to Watch

1. **Single stock > 50% of net worth**: Urgent diversification once lock-in ends
2. **No advance tax planning**: Interest under 234B/234C can be 1% per month
3. **Emotional attachment**: "Company stock will keep going up" — enforce systematic exit
4. **Market timing**: "I'll sell when it reaches Rs X" — use SWP approach instead
5. **Ignoring insider trading rules**: Promoters/KMPs must follow SEBI insider trading code
