# Liquidity Event Tax Implications — India FY 2025-26

## IPO-Related Taxation

### For Promoters / Pre-IPO Shareholders

| Event | Tax Treatment |
|-------|--------------|
| Sale through OFS (Offer for Sale) during IPO | Listed share — LTCG at 12.5% (if held >12 months from date of allotment) |
| Pre-IPO shares (held >24 months before listing) | LTCG at 12.5% from listing date if held >12 months post-listing |
| Pre-IPO shares (held <24 months) | STCG at 20% if sold within 12 months of listing |
| Allotment in IPO | No tax on allotment; tax on subsequent sale |
| Listing gains | STCG at 20% if sold on listing day (held <12 months) |

### Cost of Acquisition for Pre-IPO Shares
- If acquired before listing: Actual cost of acquisition (not IPO price)
- If allotted in IPO: IPO allotment price is cost
- No indexation benefit for listed equity LTCG (flat 12.5% rate)

## M&A / Buyout Taxation

### Share-for-Share Exchange (Tax-Neutral Merger)
| Condition | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|--------------|
| Scheme of arrangement (Sec 47(via)/(vid)) | No capital gains on exchange |
| Cost basis of new shares | Same as original shares |
| Holding period | Includes holding period of original shares |
| Condition | Must be approved under Companies Act / NCLT |

### Cash Consideration in M&A
| Aspect | Tax Treatment |
|--------|--------------|
| Cash received for shares | Capital gains tax (LTCG 12.5% or STCG 20%) |
| Earnout payments | Taxable as capital gains in year received |
| Non-compete fees | Taxable as "income from other sources" |
| Consultancy arrangements (post-exit) | Taxable as professional/salary income |

### Buyback Taxation (Post-Budget 2024)
| Aspect | Tax Treatment |
|--------|--------------|
| Before 1 Oct 2024 | 20% buyback tax paid by company; exempt in shareholder hands |
| After 1 Oct 2024 | Buyback proceeds taxable as DIVIDEND in shareholder's hands at slab rate |
| Cost recovery | Amount invested treated as capital loss (can offset capital gains) |

### Demerger
| Aspect | Tax Treatment |
|--------|--------------|
| Share allocation in demerged entity | Tax-neutral (Sec 47(vid)) |
| Cost allocation | Proportional based on net book value |
| Holding period | Continues from original shares |

## ESOP Liquidity Events

### ESOP Exercise + Sale (Detailed Flow)

| Stage | Tax Event | Rate |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Grant | No tax | - |
| Vesting | No tax | - |
| Exercise | Perquisite tax (FMV - exercise price) | Slab rate (as salary) |
| Sale (listed, >12 months from exercise) | LTCG (sale price - FMV at exercise) | 12.5% above Rs 1.25L |
| Sale (listed, <12 months from exercise) | STCG (sale price - FMV at exercise) | 20% |
| Sale (unlisted, >24 months from exercise) | LTCG | 12.5% |
| Sale (unlisted, <24 months from exercise) | STCG | Slab rate |

**Key**: Total tax = perquisite tax at exercise + capital gains tax at sale. Plan exercise timing carefully.

### ESOP Buyback by Company
- Post Oct 2024: Buyback proceeds taxable as dividend in employee's hands at slab rate
- Perquisite tax still applies at exercise
- Cost of acquisition treated as capital loss

### Secondary Sale (Pre-IPO ESOP)
- Employee sells ESOP shares to PE fund or other buyer before IPO
- Capital gains tax applies (LTCG if >24 months from exercise, else STCG)
- Company approval usually required (Right of First Refusal clause)
- Valuation: Agreed price between buyer and seller

## Cash Deployment Framework (Post-Liquidity Event)

### Immediate (Month 1-3)
| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Park in liquid fund / FD | Capital preservation while planning |
| Pay advance tax | Avoid interest (234B/234C) on capital gains |
| Engage CA for tax computation | Accurate assessment of tax liability |
| Clear high-interest debt | Immediate ROI through interest saving |

### Short-Term (Month 3-6)
| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Build emergency fund | 12-18 months expenses (larger for first-time liquidity) |
| Insurance review | Term + health + personal accident |
| Estate planning | Will, nomination, trust if large amount |
| Define goals | Retirement, home, education, philanthropy |

### Medium-Term (Month 6-12)
| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Deploy via STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) | Gradual equity entry over 6-12 months |
| Asset allocation per risk profile | Diversified portfolio construction |
| Tax-efficient structuring | HUF, trust, NPS if applicable |
| International allocation | Up to $250K via LRS |

## Concentrated Stock De-Risking
After a liquidity event, diversification strategies:
1. **Staged selling**: Sell 20-25% per quarter over 12-18 months
2. **Tax-efficient tranches**: Sell LTCG lots first (12.5%), harvest Rs 1.25L exemption yearly
3. **Covered calls**: Write OTM calls on concentrated stock for income + partial hedge (if F&O available)
4. **Pledge + diversify**: Use stock as collateral for loan, invest loan proceeds in diversified portfolio
5. **Gift to family members**: Each family member has separate LTCG exemption of Rs 1.25L
