# ESOP Taxation — India FY 2025-26

## ESOP Lifecycle & Tax Events

### Two Tax Events

**Event 1: Exercise (Perquisite Tax)**
- When employee exercises the option and acquires shares
- Taxable amount: FMV at exercise - Exercise price
- Taxed as: Salary income (perquisite under Section 17(2))
- Tax rate: Employee's applicable income tax slab
- TDS: Employer must deduct TDS on perquisite value
- FMV determination: Listed shares — closing price on exercise date; Unlisted — merchant banker valuation

**Event 2: Sale (Capital Gains Tax)**
- When employee sells the acquired shares
- Cost of acquisition: FMV on the date of exercise (not exercise price)
- Holding period: From date of exercise (allotment), not from grant date

### Tax Calculation Example

| Step | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Grant price (exercise price) | Rs 100 per share |
| FMV at exercise | Rs 500 per share |
| Sale price (after 18 months) | Rs 800 per share |
| **Perquisite at exercise** | **Rs 400/share (500 - 100)** — taxed at slab rate |
| **LTCG at sale** | **Rs 300/share (800 - 500)** — taxed at 12.5% |

## ESOP Tax Deferral for Startups

### Section 17(2)(vi) Proviso — Eligible Startups

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Eligible entity | DPIIT-recognized startup |
| Deferral period | TDS on perquisite deferred for earliest of: |
| | - 5 years from end of exercise year |
| | - Date of sale of shares |
| | - Date employee ceases employment |
| Benefit | Cash flow relief — tax not due immediately on exercise |
| Condition | Employer must still report perquisite in salary statement |

### Practical Impact
- In listed companies: Employee must pay perquisite tax in the year of exercise (no deferral)
- In DPIIT startups: Employee can defer up to 5 years or till sale (whichever earlier)
- Many employees exercise and hold — deferral prevents cash crunch at exercise

## Holding Period & Capital Gains

### Listed Company Shares (Post-Exercise)
| Holding Period | Type | Tax Rate |
|---------------|------|----------|
| Up to 12 months | STCG | 20% |
| > 12 months | LTCG | 12.5% (above Rs 1.25L) |

### Unlisted Company Shares (Post-Exercise)
| Holding Period | Type | Tax Rate |
|---------------|------|----------|
| Up to 24 months | STCG | Slab rate |
| > 24 months | LTCG | 12.5% |

## Common ESOP Scenarios

### Scenario 1: IT Professional at Listed Company
- Gets 1,000 shares, exercise price Rs 500, FMV at exercise Rs 2,000
- Perquisite: 1,000 x (2,000 - 500) = Rs 15,00,000 — taxed as salary
- At 30% slab: Rs 4,50,000 tax due on exercise (needs cash even before selling!)
- If sells at Rs 2,500 after 18 months: LTCG = 1,000 x (2,500 - 2,000) = Rs 5,00,000

### Scenario 2: Startup Employee (DPIIT Recognized)
- Gets 5,000 shares, exercise price Rs 10, FMV at exercise Rs 200
- Perquisite: 5,000 x (200 - 10) = Rs 9,50,000
- TDS deferred for 5 years or till sale (whichever earlier)
- If startup IPOs in 3 years and shares trade at Rs 1,000:
  - Perquisite tax triggered on earlier of sale date or 5th year
  - If sells at Rs 1,000: LTCG = 5,000 x (1,000 - 200) = Rs 40,00,000 at 12.5%

### Scenario 3: Shares Become Worthless
- Employee exercised ESOPs, paid perquisite tax
- Company fails, shares worth zero
- Capital loss: FMV at exercise - 0 = full loss
- Can set off: LTCL against LTCG; STCL against any capital gains
- Perquisite tax already paid is NOT refundable

## ESOP Planning Strategies

1. **Stagger exercises**: Don't exercise all in one year — spread perquisite across tax years
2. **Exercise in low-income year**: Sabbatical, career break — lower slab rate on perquisite
3. **Sell to cover**: Sell enough shares to pay perquisite tax, hold rest
4. **Tax loss harvesting**: If stock drops below exercise FMV, sell to book capital loss
5. **HUF transfer**: After exercise, gift shares to HUF (but clubbing rules apply for spouse)
6. **LAS borrowing**: Use Loan Against Securities on vested shares instead of selling
7. **Startup deferral**: If DPIIT recognized, time exit to maximize deferral benefit
