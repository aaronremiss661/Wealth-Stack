# Angel Tax Rules — Section 56(2)(viib) & Startup Benefits

## Section 56(2)(viib) — Angel Tax

### The Rule
When a closely-held company issues shares at a premium exceeding Fair Market Value (FMV), the excess is taxed as "income from other sources" in the company's hands.

### Major Update — Budget 2024
**Section 56(2)(viib) has been ABOLISHED for ALL investors effective FY 2024-25.**

| Period | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Before FY 2024-25 | Angel tax applied to resident investors; exemption for DPIIT-recognized startups |
| FY 2024-25 onwards | Section 56(2)(viib) REMOVED entirely — no angel tax for any investor |

This means:
- No angel tax on shares issued at any premium to any investor (resident or non-resident)
- Startups can raise funds at any valuation without tax concern
- Previously, non-resident investors were exempt via DTAA; now all investors are exempt
- Existing proceedings/disputes may still need resolution

### Historical Context (For Existing Cases)
- Angel tax was 30.9% on the excess over FMV
- FMV determined by DCF method (for resident investors) or merchant banker valuation
- Caused significant friction in startup fundraising
- Many startups received notices for angel tax assessment

## DPIIT Recognition — Startup India

### Eligibility for DPIIT Recognition
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Age | Incorporated < 10 years ago |
| Entity type | Pvt Ltd, LLP, or registered Partnership |
| Turnover | < Rs 100 crore in any financial year |
| Innovation | Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/services |
| Not formed by splitting | Not formed by splitting up or reconstruction of existing business |

### Benefits of DPIIT Recognition
| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Tax holiday (80-IAC) | 100% tax exemption for 3 consecutive years out of first 10 years |
| Self-certification | Compliance under 6 labour laws and 3 environmental laws |
| Fast-track patent | 80% rebate on patent filing fees, expedited examination |
| Easy wind-up | Insolvency resolution in 90 days (under IBC) |
| Fund of Funds | Access to SIDBI Fund of Funds for Startups |
| Government procurement | Relaxation of prior experience/turnover criteria |
| Tax benefit on LTCG | Section 54GB: Reinvestment of LTCG from property into eligible startup |

### Section 80-IAC Tax Holiday
| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Deduction | 100% of profits for 3 consecutive assessment years |
| Window | Any 3 years out of first 10 years from incorporation |
| Condition | Must be DPIIT recognized + certified by Inter-Ministerial Board |
| Entity | Only for Pvt Ltd companies and LLPs |
| Turnover limit | < Rs 100 crore |

## Angel Investor Practical Guide

### Valuation Methods for Early-Stage Startups
| Method | Best For | Approach |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Comparable transactions | Seed/Pre-Series A | Valuation based on similar recent deals |
| Revenue multiple | Series A/B (with revenue) | Revenue x sector multiple (5-15x for SaaS) |
| DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) | Later stage with projections | Future cash flow discounted at high rate (30-40%) |
| Berkus Method | Pre-revenue | $500K for each: idea, prototype, team, relationships, sales |
| Scorecard Method | Pre-revenue | Adjust average pre-money valuation based on factors |

### Typical Startup Investment Stages (India)

| Stage | Ticket Size | Valuation Range | Investor Type |
|-------|------------|----------------|---------------|
| Pre-seed | Rs 25L - Rs 1Cr | Rs 2-10 Cr | Angels, family |
| Seed | Rs 1-5 Cr | Rs 10-50 Cr | Angel networks, micro VCs |
| Series A | Rs 10-50 Cr | Rs 50-300 Cr | VCs |
| Series B | Rs 50-200 Cr | Rs 300-1,500 Cr | VCs, growth PE |
| Series C+ | Rs 200 Cr+ | Rs 1,500 Cr+ | Late-stage VCs, PE |

### Angel Investment Tax Treatment
| Event | Tax Treatment |
|-------|--------------|
| Investment | No angel tax (post FY 2024-25) |
| Dividend received | Taxable at slab rate |
| Sale of shares (listed, >12 months) | 12.5% LTCG above Rs 1.25L |
| Sale of shares (unlisted, >24 months) | 12.5% LTCG (post Budget 2024) |
| Sale of shares (unlisted, <24 months) | Slab rate as STCG |
| Total loss (company shuts down) | Can be set off against LTCG |
