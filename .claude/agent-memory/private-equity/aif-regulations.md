# SEBI AIF Regulations — 2024 Updates

Source: SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012 (amended through 2024)

## Registration Framework

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Application to | SEBI |
| Application fee | Rs 1,00,000 |
| Registration fee | Rs 5,00,000 (one-time) |
| Validity | Perpetual (unless surrendered or revoked) |
| Legal structure | Trust (most common), Company, LLP |
| Compliance officer | Mandatory appointment |

## Fund Structure Requirements

| Parameter | Cat I | Cat II | Cat III |
|-----------|-------|--------|---------|
| Minimum fund size | Rs 20 Cr | Rs 20 Cr | Rs 20 Cr |
| Minimum investor contribution | Rs 1 Cr | Rs 1 Cr | Rs 1 Cr |
| Manager contribution | 2.5% or Rs 5 Cr | 2.5% or Rs 5 Cr | 5% or Rs 10 Cr |
| Max investors per scheme | 1,000 | 1,000 | 1,000 |
| Employee minimum | Rs 25 L | Rs 25 L | Rs 25 L |
| Accredited investor minimum | Rs 70 L | Rs 70 L | Rs 70 L |
| Fund life | Min 3 years + 2 yr extension | Min 3 years + 2 yr extension | Open/closed |

## 2024 Key Amendments

### Large Value Fund for Accredited Investors (LVFAI)
- Minimum investment: Rs 70 lakh (instead of Rs 1 Cr)
- Only accredited investors eligible
- Lighter regulatory compliance
- Flexible investment restrictions
- No concentration limits from SEBI (self-governed)

### Performance Benchmarking
- SEBI mandates AIFs to declare benchmark at inception
- Performance to be reported net-of-fees against benchmark
- Annual report to investors with benchmark comparison

### Dematerialization
- AIF units must be in demat form
- Mandatory for all new schemes
- Existing schemes must transition

### Valuation Norms
- Independent valuer mandatory for unlisted investments
- Valuation at least once every 6 months (Cat I/II)
- Cat III: More frequent (quarterly or as per PPM)
- SEBI-approved valuation methodologies (DCF, comparable, asset-based)

## Investment Restrictions

### Category I
| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Single investee company | Max 25% of investable funds |
| Leverage | Not permitted |
| Listed investments | Permitted (but focus on unlisted) |
| Associates | Max 25% of corpus |

### Category II
| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Single investee company | Max 25% of investable funds |
| Leverage | Not permitted (only for meeting temporary 30-day requirements) |
| Listed investments | Permitted |
| Borrowing | Only for meeting temporary shortfalls (max 30 days, 4 times/year) |

### Category III
| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Single investee company | Max 10% of investable funds |
| Leverage | Permitted (max 2x NAV) |
| Short selling | Permitted |
| Derivatives | Hedging and trading permitted |
| Borrowing | Permitted up to 2x |

## Reporting & Compliance

| Obligation | Frequency | To Whom |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| NAV reporting | Monthly (Cat III), Quarterly (Cat I/II) | SEBI + Investors |
| Financial statements | Annual (audited) | SEBI + Investors |
| Compliance certificate | Annual | SEBI |
| Material change report | Within 15 days of event | SEBI |
| PPM (Private Placement Memo) | At launch + material amendments | Investors |
| Contribution/distribution report | At each event | Investors |

## Investor Protection

| Protection | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Segregated portfolio | Side-pocketing for distressed assets allowed |
| Key person clause | If key person exits, investors get exit option |
| Winding-up provision | Mandatory dissolution clause in PPM |
| Investor advisory committee | Recommended (not mandatory) for governance |
| Dispute resolution | Arbitration clause mandatory in PPM |
| SEBI SCORES | Investor grievance mechanism |

## Recent SEBI Focus Areas (2024-2025)
1. Greenwashing in ESG-themed AIFs
2. Valuation practices (inflated NAVs in unlisted portfolios)
3. Related-party transactions within AIF groups
4. Evergreening of investments between AIFs of same manager
5. Mis-selling to non-sophisticated investors
6. Liquidity management in open-ended Cat III AIFs
