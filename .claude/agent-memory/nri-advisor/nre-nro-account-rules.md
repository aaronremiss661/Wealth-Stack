# NRE / NRO / FCNR(B) Account Rules

## Account Comparison

| Feature | NRE | NRO | FCNR(B) |
|---------|-----|-----|---------|
| Full form | Non-Resident External | Non-Resident Ordinary | Foreign Currency Non-Resident (Banks) |
| Currency | INR | INR | Foreign currency (USD/GBP/EUR/JPY/CAD/AUD) |
| Source of funds | Foreign earnings only | India-sourced income (rent, dividends, pension) + foreign earnings | Foreign earnings only |
| Joint holding | Only with another NRI | With resident Indian allowed | Only with another NRI |
| Repatriability | Fully repatriable | Up to USD 1M/yr (after tax) | Fully repatriable |
| Interest taxation in India | Tax-free | 30% TDS (+ surcharge + cess) | Tax-free |
| Types available | Savings, FD, RD | Savings, FD, RD | Only FD (1-5 year tenure) |
| Currency risk | Yes (INR fluctuation) | Yes (INR fluctuation) | No (held in foreign currency) |

## NRE Account Details

- **Interest rates (FD)**: 6.5-7.5% (varies by bank, tenure)
- **Savings rate**: 2.5-4%
- **Tax status**: Interest is fully exempt under Section 10(4)(ii) — no TDS, no inclusion in ITR
- **On return to India**: Must be redesignated as resident account or RFC account
- **Loan against NRE FD**: Available up to 90% of deposit value
- **Power of Attorney**: Resident Indian can operate NRE account via PoA (limited operations — withdrawals for local payments only)

## NRO Account Details

- **Interest rates (FD)**: 6.5-7.5% (similar to resident FD rates)
- **TDS on interest**: 30% + applicable surcharge + 4% cess
- **DTAA benefit**: Can claim lower TDS rate (e.g., 15% for US NRIs) with TRC + Form 10F
- **Repatriation**: Max USD 1M per financial year after paying all Indian taxes
- **Form 15CA/15CB**: Required for repatriation above Rs 5 lakh
- **Credits allowed**: Rent, dividends, pension, matured FD, sale proceeds of assets
- **On return to India**: Automatically becomes regular resident savings/FD account

## FCNR(B) Account Details

- **Currencies**: USD, GBP, EUR, JPY, CAD, AUD
- **Tenure**: Minimum 1 year, maximum 5 years
- **Interest rates**: USD deposits ~4.5-5.5%, GBP deposits ~4-5% (varies by bank)
- **Tax status**: Interest fully exempt — same as NRE
- **Key advantage**: Zero currency risk (principal + interest in foreign currency)
- **Premature withdrawal**: Allowed with penalty (lower interest rate)
- **Popular strategy**: Park large sums in FCNR(B) when expecting INR depreciation

## RFC (Resident Foreign Currency) Account

- For returning NRIs who have become residents
- Can hold foreign currency balances from erstwhile NRE/FCNR accounts
- Interest is taxable in India (unlike NRE)
- Fully repatriable
- Useful during RNOR period for parking foreign assets

## RNOR (Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident) Status

### Eligibility (any ONE condition):
- NRI in 9 out of 10 preceding years, OR
- In India for 729 days or less in preceding 7 years

### RNOR Benefits (up to 2 years typically):
- Foreign income NOT taxable in India
- Only India-sourced income taxable
- Ideal window to bring back overseas investments
- Convert PFIC holdings (US NRIs) before becoming Ordinary Resident
- Can continue NRE/FCNR accounts during RNOR period

### Action Items During RNOR Window:
1. Sell overseas mutual funds (avoid PFIC issues post-RNOR)
2. Liquidate foreign retirement accounts if beneficial
3. Repatriate FCNR(B) deposits (interest still tax-free during RNOR)
4. Convert NRE → RFC or resident account
5. Update KYC and residential status with all financial institutions
