# DTAA Treaty Rates — Key NRI Corridors

Source: India's Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements (updated through 2024)

## India-US DTAA

| Income Type | India Tax Rate | US Tax Rate | Treaty Rate |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Dividends | Slab rate | Up to 20% | 15% (25% if >10% holding) |
| Interest | Slab rate | Up to 37% | 15% |
| Royalties | Slab rate | 30% | 15% |
| Capital gains (equity) | 12.5% LTCG / 20% STCG | Ordinary income | As per domestic law (no relief) |
| Capital gains (property) | 12.5% LTCG | 0-20% | Taxed in country where property is |

- Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) available in both countries to avoid double taxation
- US citizens/green card holders: taxed on worldwide income regardless of residence
- Form 10F required to claim DTAA benefits in India
- Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from country of residence mandatory

## India-UAE DTAA (Revised 2024)

| Income Type | Treaty Rate |
|-------------|-------------|
| Dividends | 0% (if beneficial owner holds 10%+ shares) |
| Interest | 0% |
| Royalties/FTS | 0% |
| Capital gains (shares) | Taxed only in country of residence |

- UAE has introduced 9% corporate tax (June 2023) but no personal income tax
- NRE interest remains 100% tax-free for UAE NRIs
- Most favorable DTAA corridor for Indian NRIs
- Capital gains on shares: taxed in India only if NRI sells India-listed shares (domestic law applies)

## India-UK DTAA

| Income Type | Treaty Rate |
|-------------|-------------|
| Dividends | 15% (10% if beneficial owner holds 10%+ shares) |
| Interest | 15% |
| Royalties | 15% |
| Capital gains (shares) | Taxed in country of residence (for listed shares) |

- UK taxes worldwide income for UK tax residents
- Remittance basis of taxation available for non-domiciled UK residents
- UK CGT applies to worldwide gains for UK residents
- Form 10F + TRC required for DTAA benefits

## India-Singapore DTAA (Revised 2017)

| Income Type | Treaty Rate |
|-------------|-------------|
| Dividends | 15% (10% if holding 25%+ shares) |
| Interest | 15% |
| Royalties | 10% |
| Capital gains (shares) | Taxed in source country (India) |

- Post-2017 amendment: Capital gains on Indian shares are taxable in India
- Earlier treaty gave Singapore-route a tax advantage (removed)
- Limitation of Benefits (LOB) clause applies
- Singapore has no CGT, so effective single taxation in India

## Key DTAA Compliance Requirements

1. **Tax Residency Certificate (TRC)**: Must be obtained from country of residence
2. **Form 10F**: Self-declaration filed with income tax authority in India
3. **PAN**: Mandatory for claiming DTAA benefit (else higher TDS applies)
4. **No-PE certificate**: For business income, prove no Permanent Establishment in India
5. **Section 90/90A**: Domestic law provision enabling DTAA benefit claim

## TDS Rates for NRIs (Without DTAA)

| Income Type | TDS Rate |
|-------------|----------|
| Equity LTCG (>12 months) | 12.5% + surcharge + cess |
| Equity STCG (<12 months) | 20% + surcharge + cess |
| Debt fund gains | 20% + surcharge + cess (LTCG) / 30% (STCG) |
| Interest (FD/NRO) | 30% + surcharge + cess |
| Rent | 30% + surcharge + cess |
| Professional fees | 10% |
| Dividend | 20% + surcharge + cess |

Higher TDS (20%) applies if NRI does not have PAN.
