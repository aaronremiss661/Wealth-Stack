# FEMA & Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) Rules

Source: RBI Master Direction on LRS (updated 2024), FEMA 1999

## Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

### Basic Limits
- **Annual limit**: USD 2,50,000 per financial year per resident individual
- Applies to: all current and capital account transactions
- Minor children: separate limit of USD 2,50,000 (guardian to remit)
- Corporates/partnership firms/HUFs/trusts: NOT eligible for LRS

### Permitted LRS Transactions
- International education expenses
- Medical treatment abroad
- Private visits / tourism
- Gifts and donations
- Investment in equity/debt/property abroad
- Opening foreign currency accounts abroad
- Maintenance of close relatives abroad
- Investment in Joint Ventures / Wholly Owned Subsidiaries abroad

### TCS on LRS Remittances (FY 2025-26)

| Purpose | TCS Rate | Threshold |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Education (funded by loan) | 0.5% | Above Rs 7 lakh |
| Education (self-funded) | 5% | Above Rs 7 lakh |
| Medical treatment | 5% | Above Rs 7 lakh |
| Tour packages | 5% | Above Rs 7 lakh |
| All other purposes (investment, gifts) | 20% | Above Rs 7 lakh |

- First Rs 7 lakh remittance in a FY: No TCS (across all purposes combined)
- TCS is adjustable against income tax liability (not an additional tax)
- TCS certificate (Form 27D) can be used for credit in ITR

### LRS Documentation
- PAN card (mandatory)
- Form A2 (application for outward remittance)
- Self-declaration of purpose
- Bank may ask for supporting documents (university admission letter, medical records, etc.)

## NRO Account Repatriation

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Annual limit | USD 1,000,000 per financial year |
| Tax clearance | All India taxes must be paid before repatriation |
| Form 15CA | Online submission by remitter to Income Tax portal |
| Form 15CB | CA certificate (required if remittance > Rs 5 lakh per transaction) |
| Source of funds | Must be from legitimate NRO credits (rent, dividends, pension, etc.) |

## FEMA Investment Rules for NRIs

### Equity Investments (PIS — Portfolio Investment Scheme)
- NRI can invest up to 5% of paid-up capital of any Indian listed company
- Aggregate NRI limit: 10% per company (can be raised to 24% by company board)
- Must route through designated bank account (NRE/NRO)
- Delivery-based only (no intraday trading for NRIs)
- F&O trading: Allowed for NRIs on repatriation basis through NRE account

### Mutual Fund Investments
- Allowed for NRIs (repatriable via NRE, non-repatriable via NRO)
- US/Canada NRIs: Many AMCs restrict due to FATCA/PFIC compliance
- AMCs accepting US NRIs (limited): Birla Sun Life, UTI, PPFAS, SBI MF (selected schemes)
- KYC for NRI MF investment: PAN + overseas address proof + FATCA self-certification

### Real Estate
- NRIs CAN buy: Residential and commercial property (no limit on number)
- NRIs CANNOT buy: Agricultural land, plantation property, farmhouse
- Payment: Must be through NRE/NRO/FCNR account (no foreign currency)
- Sale proceeds: Repatriable if purchased from NRE funds

### Prohibited Investments for NRIs
- Agricultural land / farmhouse / plantation property
- Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs)
- New PPF accounts (existing continue till maturity)
- Nidhi company deposits
- Chit funds
