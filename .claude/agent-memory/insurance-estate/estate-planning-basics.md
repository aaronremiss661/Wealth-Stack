# Estate Planning Basics — India

## Key Estate Planning Instruments

### 1. Will
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Type | Privileged or Unprivileged Will |
| Requirement | Sound mind, 2 witnesses, signed on each page |
| Registration | Optional but strongly recommended (Sub-Registrar) |
| Cost | Rs 500-5,000 (registration) + lawyer fees |
| Revocation | Can be changed anytime during lifetime |
| Probate | Required in Kolkata, Chennai, Mumbai (High Court jurisdiction) |
| Executor | Named person responsible for executing the will |

**Best Practices**:
- Register the will to avoid disputes
- Update after major life events (marriage, child birth, death of beneficiary)
- Store copy with lawyer, family member, and bank locker
- Video record the will-signing as additional evidence

### 2. Nomination vs Will

| Aspect | Nomination | Will |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Purpose | Facilitates quick transfer | Determines final ownership |
| Legal standing | Trustee/custodian role only | Overrides nomination |
| Applicable to | Bank accounts, MFs, insurance, demat, FDs | All assets |
| Who gets it | Nominee receives as custodian | Beneficiary as per will |
| Disputes | Will beneficiary can claim from nominee | Final authority |

**Critical point**: Nomination does NOT equal ownership. The will always prevails.

### 3. Trust Structures

| Trust Type | Purpose | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Private Trust (revocable) | Flexible, can be dissolved | Income taxed at settlor's slab |
| Private Trust (irrevocable) | Asset protection, succession | Taxed at maximum marginal rate (42.7%) if beneficiaries not specified |
| Specific beneficiary trust | Clear distribution | Taxed in beneficiary's hands |
| Discretionary trust | Trustee decides distribution | Taxed at maximum marginal rate |
| Charitable trust (Sec 12A) | Philanthropy | Exempt if 85% of income spent on charitable purposes |

### 4. HUF (Hindu Undivided Family)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Applicability | Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, Sikhs |
| Tax status | Separate PAN, separate tax entity |
| Creation | Automatically exists by birth; formally created by throwing assets into common pool |
| Karta | Eldest male/female member (post 2016, female can be Karta) |
| Partition | Can be partial or total; partition deed required |
| Tax benefits | Separate 80C (Rs 1.5L), separate LTCG exemption (Rs 1.25L) |
| Succession | Governed by Hindu Succession Act, 2005 (equal rights to daughters) |

## Succession Laws in India

### Hindu Succession Act, 2005 (Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, Sikhs)
- **Class I heirs**: Widow, sons, daughters, mother (equal share)
- **Daughters**: Equal coparcenary rights (2005 amendment)
- **Ancestral property**: Daughter has equal right by birth
- **Self-acquired property**: Owner can will to anyone
- **Intestate (no will)**: Distributed equally among Class I heirs

### Indian Succession Act, 1925 (Christians, Parsis)
- **Christian**: Widow gets 1/3, children get 2/3 (if no will)
- **Parsi**: Widow and children get equal shares

### Muslim Personal Law
- **Only 1/3** of estate can be willed; rest follows Sharia succession
- **Heirs**: Specific shares defined (wife, sons, daughters, parents)
- No concept of will for entire estate

## Asset-Wise Transfer on Death

| Asset | Transfer Mechanism | Documents Needed |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Bank account | Nomination → Succession certificate/Will | Death certificate, nominee ID |
| Mutual funds | Nomination → Transmission form | Death certificate, KYC of nominee |
| Demat shares | Nomination → Transmission | Death certificate, affidavit |
| Insurance | Nominee (absolute for insurance) | Death certificate, claim form |
| Property | Will / succession certificate / legal heir certificate | Probate (if applicable), mutation |
| EPF/PPF | Nomination form | Death certificate |
| NPS | Nominee as per subscriber registration | Death certificate |
| Gold (physical) | Possession + will | Will |

## Estate Planning Checklist

1. Written and registered will
2. All accounts and investments have updated nominations
3. Life insurance beneficiary designations current
4. Power of Attorney (financial + medical) executed
5. HUF formalized (if Hindu) with proper deed
6. Trust created (if net worth > Rs 5Cr or complex family)
7. Digital asset inventory (passwords, crypto keys)
8. Business succession plan (if business owner)
9. Communicate plan to family / trusted advisor
10. Review every 2-3 years or after major life event
