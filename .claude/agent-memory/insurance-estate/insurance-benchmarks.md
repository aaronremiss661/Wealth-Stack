# Insurance Benchmarks — India FY 2025-26

## Term Insurance

### Coverage Guidelines
- **Minimum cover**: 10x annual income (basic)
- **Recommended cover**: 15-20x annual income (comprehensive)
- **Factor in**: Outstanding loans + future goals (education, wedding) + 5 years of expenses

## Health Insurance

### Minimum Coverage Guidelines

| Family Size | Minimum Sum Insured | Recommended |
|------------|--------------------:|------------:|
| Individual (young, no dependents) | Rs 5 lakh | Rs 10 lakh |
| Couple (no children) | Rs 10 lakh | Rs 15-20 lakh |
| Family of 4 (2 adults + 2 kids) | Rs 10 lakh | Rs 20-25 lakh |
| Senior citizen (60+) | Rs 10 lakh | Rs 25-50 lakh |
| HNI family | Rs 50 lakh | Rs 1 crore+ |

### Super Top-Up Strategy
- Base policy: Rs 5-10 lakh (employer or individual)
- Super top-up: Rs 25-50 lakh with deductible = base sum insured
- Total effective cover: Rs 30-60 lakh
- Super top-up premium (Rs 25L cover, Rs 5L deductible, age 35): Rs 4,000-6,000/year
- This is the most cost-effective way to get high coverage

### Health Insurance Key Features to Check
1. Room rent limits (prefer no capping or min Rs 10K/day)
2. Pre-existing disease waiting period (2-4 years standard)
3. Co-payment clause (avoid if possible, common in senior plans)
4. Restoration benefit (doubles sum insured after full claim)
5. No-claim bonus (NCB): 10-50% increase per claim-free year
6. Day care procedures coverage (500+ procedures)
7. AYUSH treatment coverage
8. Maternity coverage (if needed, usually 2-4 year waiting)
