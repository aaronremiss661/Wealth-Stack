# FEMA & RBI Rules for NRI Investments

> Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 & RBI Master Directions.
> Applicable: FY 2025-26. Last updated: 2026-03-25.

---

## 1. Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

**Applicable to**: Resident Indians (not NRIs — NRIs have separate repatriation rules).

### Key Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Annual limit | **USD 250,000** per financial year (April-March) |
| Per person | Each individual (including minors through guardian) |
| Clubbing | No clubbing — each family member has separate limit |
| TCS (Tax Collected at Source) | 20% on remittance above Rs 7 lakh (5% for education loan) |

### Permitted Purposes

- **Investment**: Equity, debt, mutual funds, real estate abroad.
- **Gifts & donations**: To NRIs/foreign nationals.
- **Education**: Tuition, living expenses abroad.
- **Medical**: Treatment abroad.
- **Travel**: Business/personal travel expenses.
- **Maintenance of relatives**: Abroad.
- **Property purchase**: Residential/commercial property abroad.

### Not Permitted Under LRS

- Remittance to countries identified as non-cooperative by FATF.
- Purchase of lottery/sweepstakes tickets.
- Trading on foreign exchange on margin (forex trading).
- Capital account remittances to Pakistan, Nepal, Bhutan (separate channels).

### TCS on LRS (Section 206C(1G))

| Purpose | TCS Rate | Threshold |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Education (funded by loan) | 0.5% above Rs 7 lakh | Rs 7 lakh |
| Education (self-funded) | 5% above Rs 7 lakh | Rs 7 lakh |
| Medical treatment | 5% above Rs 7 lakh | Rs 7 lakh |
| All other purposes (including investment) | **20%** above Rs 7 lakh | Rs 7 lakh |
| Overseas tour package | 5% up to Rs 7 lakh; 20% above | Rs 7 lakh |

- TCS is **adjustable** against total income tax liability — it is not an additional tax, just advance collection.
- Can be claimed as refund if total tax liability is lower.

## 2. NRE Account (Non-Resident External)

### Features

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Currency | Indian Rupees (funded by foreign currency) |
| Source of funds | Only foreign earnings / remittances from abroad |
| Joint holding | Only with another NRI (not with resident) |
| Repatriation | **Freely repatriable** — principal + interest |
| Interest taxability in India | **Tax-free** (Section 10(4)(ii)) |
| FD rates | Approximately 6-7% p.a. (varies by bank, lower than NRO) |
| Types | Savings, Current, FD, Recurring Deposit |
| Premature FD closure | Permitted with penalty (typically 0.5-1%) |

### Operational Rules

- NRE account **must be redesignated** to resident account upon return to India (within reasonable time).
- Funds in NRE can be transferred to NRO but NOT vice versa (NRO to NRE not allowed).
- Power of Attorney: NRI can give PoA to resident Indian for operating NRE account (limited operations — no repatriation via PoA).
- NRE FD can be used as collateral for loans in India.

### Investment from NRE (Repatriable Basis)

- Equity shares (via PIS route or IPO).
- Mutual funds.
- NPS (National Pension System).
- Government securities.
- Bonds/debentures of Indian companies.

## 3. NRO Account (Non-Resident Ordinary)

### Features

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Currency | Indian Rupees |
| Source of funds | Indian income (rent, dividends, pension, interest) + remittances |
| Joint holding | With NRI or resident Indian |
| Repatriation | **Restricted** — up to USD 1,000,000 per financial year (after tax clearance) |
| Interest taxability | **Taxable at 30%** + surcharge + cess (for NRIs) |
| FD rates | Approximately 6.5-7.5% p.a. |
| TDS on interest | 30% + applicable surcharge and cess |

### Repatriation from NRO

- Maximum **USD 1,000,000 per financial year** (net of applicable taxes).
- **Form 15CA**: Online undertaking by remitter (through income tax portal).
- **Form 15CB**: Certificate from Chartered Accountant certifying tax compliance and nature of remittance.
- Required for remittances exceeding Rs 5 lakh.
- **DTAA benefit**: Can claim lower TDS under DTAA with Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from resident country.

### NRO Use Cases

- Rent received from Indian property.
- Dividends from Indian company holdings.
- Interest from Indian deposits.
- Pension income.
- Maturity proceeds of investments made from Indian income.

## 4. FEMA Residential Status

### Definition (Different from Income Tax Act)

| Status | FEMA Definition |
|--------|----------------|
| Person Resident in India | Person residing in India for more than 182 days during the preceding financial year |
| Person Resident Outside India (NRI) | Indian citizen who does not satisfy 182-day test |
| PIO/OCI | Person of Indian Origin / Overseas Citizen of India — treated at par with NRI for most FEMA purposes |

### Key Difference from Income Tax

| Aspect | FEMA | Income Tax Act |
|--------|------|---------------|
| Purpose | Foreign exchange transactions | Tax liability |
| Residential status | Binary: Resident or NRI | Resident, RNOR, Non-Resident |
| RNOR concept | Does not exist in FEMA | Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident — special status for returning NRIs (2-3 years) |
| Determination | Based on preceding FY | Based on current FY |

**Practical impact**: A person can be NRI under FEMA but RNOR under Income Tax — different rules apply for investment permissions (FEMA) vs. tax liability (IT Act).

## 5. NRI Investment in India — What's Allowed

### Equity

| Route | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| PIS (Portfolio Investment Scheme) | NRI buys/sells listed shares on stock exchange via designated bank. Maximum 5% of paid-up capital per NRI; aggregate NRI limit 10% (extendable to 24% with board resolution). |
| IPO/FPO | Direct application; no PIS needed |
| Unlisted equity | Under FDI route (automatic/government) based on sector |
| Repatriation | Repatriable if purchased from NRE/FCNR; non-repatriable if from NRO |

### Mutual Funds

- NRIs can invest in Indian mutual funds (both equity and debt).
- **Repatriable**: If invested from NRE/FCNR account.
- **Non-repatriable**: If invested from NRO account.
- **US/Canada NRIs**: Many AMCs restrict investments due to FATCA/CRS compliance — only select AMCs accept (e.g., UTI, SBI MF, PPFAS for US NRIs).

### NPS (National Pension System)

- NRIs can open NPS account.
- Contributions from NRE/NRO account.
- **Repatriation**: Maturity proceeds repatriable (subject to FEMA conditions).
- Account becomes dormant if NRI status changes to non-citizen (OCI/PIO can continue).

### Real Estate

| What NRI CAN Buy | What NRI CANNOT Buy |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Residential property (any number, but repatriation of sale proceeds limited to **2 properties**) | Agricultural land |
| Commercial property (any number) | Plantation property |
| | Farmhouse |

- **Sale proceeds repatriation**: Maximum 2 residential properties' sale proceeds can be repatriated (up to USD 1M per FY from NRO).
- **Lock-in for repatriation**: Property must be held for at least 3 years if purchased from NRE funds for full repatriation.
- **TDS on sale**: Buyer must deduct TDS at 12.5% (LTCG) or 30% (STCG) from NRI seller — NRI can apply for lower TDS under Section 197.

## 6. FPI/FDI Limits

### FPI (Foreign Portfolio Investment) Limits

| Parameter | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Individual FPI per company | 10% of paid-up capital |
| Aggregate FPI per company | 24% (default, extendable to sectoral cap via board resolution) |
| Government securities | No limit (subject to overall FPI debt limit) |

### FDI Sectoral Caps

| Sector | Automatic Route | Government Route |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Most manufacturing, IT, e-commerce (marketplace) | 100% | - |
| Insurance | 74% | - |
| Defense | 74% (automatic) | Up to 100% (govt) |
| Telecom | 100% (automatic up to 49%, govt above) | Above 49% |
| Media (print) | 26% (govt) | - |
| Multi-brand retail | - | 51% (govt) |
| Banking (private) | 49% (automatic) | 74% (govt) |
| Broadcasting | 49-100% depending on type | Varies |

## 7. DTAA — Key Treaty Rates

### Dividend Withholding Tax Rates Under DTAA

| Country | DTAA Rate on Dividends | Domestic Rate | Effective Rate |
|---------|----------------------|---------------|---------------|
| USA | 15% (25% if >10% holding) | 20% | 15% (with DTAA) |
| UAE | 10% | 20% | 10% |
| UK | 15% (10% if >10% holding) | 20% | 10-15% |
| Singapore | 10% (15% if >25% holding) | 20% | 10-15% |
| Canada | 15% (25% if >10% holding) | 20% | 15% |
| Australia | 15% | 20% | 15% |

### Interest Income — Treaty Rates

| Country | DTAA Rate on Interest | Notes |
|---------|----------------------|-------|
| USA | 15% | 10% for banks |
| UAE | 5-12.5% | Depends on payer type |
| UK | 15% | 10% for banks |
| Singapore | 15% | 10% for banks/FI |
| Canada | 15% | |
| Australia | 15% | |

### Capital Gains — Treaty Treatment

- Most Indian DTAAs allow **source country taxation** for capital gains on shares (India can tax NRI's equity gains).
- **India-Singapore DTAA**: Capital gains on shares taxable in India (LOB clause added 2017).
- **India-Mauritius DTAA**: Capital gains on shares acquired after April 2017 taxable in India (grandfathering ended).
- **India-UAE DTAA**: No capital gains article — India's domestic law applies.

### How to Claim DTAA Benefit

1. Obtain **Tax Residency Certificate (TRC)** from country of residence.
2. Submit TRC + **Form 10F** to Indian payer/deductor.
3. Provide **self-declaration** of no PE (Permanent Establishment) in India.
4. Lower TDS applied at source if documentation submitted in time; else claim refund via ITR.
5. In resident country: Claim **Foreign Tax Credit** for taxes paid in India (Section 90/91 of Indian IT Act for Indian resident; corresponding provision in resident country law for NRI).

## 8. Repatriation Summary

| Source | Repatriation | Documentation |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| NRE account (savings/FD) | Freely repatriable | Bank's own process |
| NRO account | Up to USD 1M per FY | Form 15CA + 15CB (CA certificate) |
| Equity sale (PIS, from NRE) | Freely repatriable | Bank + broker confirmation |
| Equity sale (from NRO) | Within USD 1M NRO limit | Form 15CA + 15CB |
| MF redemption (NRE invested) | Freely repatriable | AMC + bank process |
| MF redemption (NRO invested) | Within USD 1M NRO limit | Form 15CA + 15CB |
| Property sale (up to 2 residential) | Within USD 1M NRO limit per FY | 15CA + 15CB + TDS compliance |
| Inheritance/gift | Up to USD 1M per FY from NRO | 15CA + 15CB + succession certificate/will |
| NPS maturity | Repatriable | NPS Trust process |
