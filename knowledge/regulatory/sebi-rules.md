# SEBI Regulations for Wealth Advisory

> Securities and Exchange Board of India — Comprehensive regulatory reference.
> Applicable as of FY 2025-26. Last updated: 2026-03-25.

---

## 1. Registered Investment Adviser (RIA) Regulations

**Governing law**: SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013 (amended 2020)

### Registration Requirements

- **Individual RIA**: Net worth minimum Rs 5 lakh, qualification (CFA/CFP/post-graduate in finance + NISM-XA/XB), experience 5 years in financial services (relaxed to 2 years with relevant PG degree).
- **Non-individual RIA**: Net worth minimum Rs 50 lakh, principal officer must meet individual qualifications, compliance officer mandatory.
- **Registration fee**: Rs 10,000 (individual), Rs 5,00,000 (non-individual body corporate), Rs 2,00,000 (non-individual partnership/LLP).
- **Renewal**: Every 5 years (was 3 years, extended in 2020 amendment).
- **BASL membership**: Mandatory registration with BSE Administration & Supervision Limited.

### Fee Structure Caps

| Fee Model | Individual Client | Non-Individual Client |
|-----------|------------------|-----------------------|
| AUM-based (% of AUM per annum) | Max 2.5% p.a. | Max 2.5% p.a. |
| Fixed fee (per annum per family) | Max Rs 75,000 | Max Rs 1,50,000 |

- **Mode choice**: RIA must choose either AUM-based OR fixed fee — cannot mix for same client.
- **Fee collection**: Must be via banking channels only, no cash.
- **Advance fee**: Cannot collect more than 2 quarters in advance.

### Client Level Agreement (CLA)

- Mandatory written agreement before providing advice.
- Must specify: services, fee structure, tenure, risk profiling method, grievance mechanism.
- Risk profiling: Mandatory risk assessment of client, documented, reviewed annually.
- Suitability: Every recommendation must be suitable for the client's risk profile — documented rationale required.

### Conflict of Interest Norms

- **Segregation**: RIA cannot be a distributor (complete segregation of advisory and distribution).
- **Disclosure**: Must disclose all conflicts — holding in recommended securities, commission from any entity.
- **No commission**: RIA cannot receive trail/commission from AMCs or product manufacturers.
- **Family disclosure**: Must disclose if RIA or family members hold the recommended security.

### Compliance & Reporting

- Maintain records for minimum 5 years (was 10, reduced in amendment).
- KYC of all clients mandatory.
- Quarterly compliance reporting to SEBI.
- Annual audit by CA/CS.

## 2. Portfolio Management Services (PMS) Regulations

**Governing law**: SEBI (Portfolio Managers) Regulations, 2020

### Key Parameters

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Minimum investment per client | Rs 50,00,000 (50 lakh) |
| Minimum net worth of portfolio manager | Rs 5,00,00,000 (5 crore) |
| Registration fee | Rs 10,00,000 (10 lakh) |
| Types | Discretionary, Non-discretionary, Advisory |

### Types of PMS

1. **Discretionary**: Portfolio manager makes all buy/sell decisions independently. Most common for HNIs.
2. **Non-discretionary**: Manager advises, client approves each transaction before execution.
3. **Advisory**: Manager only advises, client executes through own broker. No custody.

### Fee Structures

- **Fixed fee**: Typically 1-3% p.a. of AUM. Charged quarterly.
- **Performance fee (profit-sharing)**: Subject to high-water mark principle — fee only on profits above previous highest NAV.
- **Hybrid**: Combination of lower fixed + performance above hurdle rate.
- **Exit load**: Permitted, must be disclosed upfront. Typically 1-3% in first year, reducing to 0.

### Portfolio Disclosure

- Monthly portfolio statement to client within 10 days of month-end.
- Audited annual statement.
- Performance reporting: Must use TWRR (Time Weighted Rate of Return).
- Benchmark comparison mandatory.

### Client Segregation

- Each client's securities held in individual demat account (client's name).
- No pooling of client funds (unlike mutual funds).
- Direct ownership — securities are in client's name, not PMS provider's name.

### Tax Implications (cross-ref income-tax-rules.md)

- **Critical difference from MF**: Each buy/sell in PMS is a separate taxable event.
- Frequent churn = higher short-term capital gains = higher tax drag.
- Dividends received in PMS portfolio taxed at slab rate in client's hands.

## 3. Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) Regulations

**Governing law**: SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012 (amended periodically)

### Category I — Positive Externality Funds

| Sub-category | Focus | Government incentives |
|-------------|-------|----------------------|
| Venture Capital Funds | Startups, early-stage | Tax pass-through (Section 115UB) |
| SME Funds | Small & Medium Enterprises | SIDBI co-investment |
| Social Venture Funds | Social impact investing | CSR eligible |
| Infrastructure Funds | Infrastructure projects | Tax benefits under specific sections |

- **Tenure**: Close-ended, minimum 3 years.
- **Tax**: Pass-through status — income (other than business income) taxed in investor's hands.

### Category II — No Specific Incentives or Restrictions

| Sub-category | Focus |
|-------------|-------|
| Private Equity Funds | Growth/buyout PE |
| Debt Funds | Structured credit, performing credit |
| Fund of Funds | Investing in other AIFs |
| Real Estate Funds | Commercial/residential RE |

- **Tenure**: Close-ended, minimum 3 years.
- **Tax**: Pass-through status for income (other than business income).
- **No leverage**: Cannot borrow except for meeting temporary shortfall (max 30 days).

### Category III — Hedge Funds / Complex Strategies

- Long-short equity, derivatives strategies, algorithmic trading.
- **Open-ended or close-ended**: Flexible.
- **Leverage**: Permitted (can borrow and use derivatives for leverage).
- **Tax**: NO pass-through — fund-level taxation. Income taxed at maximum marginal rate in the fund.
- **Angel tax concern**: Not applicable post-2024 budget amendments.

### Common AIF Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Minimum investment per investor | Rs 1,00,00,000 (1 crore) |
| Minimum fund corpus | Rs 20,00,00,000 (20 crore) |
| Maximum investors per scheme | 1,000 |
| Manager/sponsor commitment | Minimum 2.5% of corpus or Rs 5 crore (whichever lower) for Cat I/II; 5% or Rs 10 crore for Cat III |
| Extension of tenure | Up to 2 years with 2/3 investor approval |

### Large Value Fund for Accredited Investors

- Minimum investment Rs 70 crore per investor (accredited).
- Relaxed diversification norms.
- Flexible investment restrictions.

## 4. Mutual Fund Regulations

**Governing law**: SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996 (extensively amended)

### Expense Ratio Caps (TER — Total Expense Ratio)

| AUM Slab | Equity Schemes (Regular) | Debt Schemes (Regular) |
|----------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| First Rs 500 crore | 2.25% | 2.00% |
| Next Rs 250 crore | 2.00% | 1.75% |
| Next Rs 1,250 crore | 1.75% | 1.50% |
| Next Rs 3,000 crore | 1.60% | 1.35% |
| Next Rs 5,000 crore | TER reduction of 0.05% for every Rs 5,000 crore | Same |
| Remaining | 1.05% | 0.80% |

- **Direct plans**: No distributor commission — TER is approximately 0.8-1.2% lower than regular plans.
- **Direct equity TER typical**: 0.3% - 1.05% depending on AUM.
- **Additional 30 bps**: Allowed for inflows from B30 cities (beyond top 30 cities).

### NFO (New Fund Offer) Rules

- NFO period: Minimum 15 days, maximum 30 days (open-ended); up to 15 days for close-ended.
- Minimum subscription: Rs 20 crore for open-ended equity, Rs 20 crore for debt.
- Allotment within 5 business days of NFO closure.
- New fund must be distinct from existing schemes (no duplication).

### Exit Load Norms

- Exit load permitted only for open-ended schemes.
- No exit load on bonus units and units allotted on reinvestment of dividends.
- Liquid funds: Graded exit load for first 7 days (Day 1: 0.0070%, Day 7: 0.0045%).
- ELSS: 3-year mandatory lock-in, no exit load after lock-in.

### Side-Pocketing

- Allowed for segregation of distressed assets (credit events rated below investment grade).
- Protects existing investors from forced selling at distressed prices.
- Segregated portfolio units listed on exchange for price discovery.
- Trigger: Credit event (downgrade to below investment grade, default).

### Stress Testing (Debt Funds)

- Large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap funds must publish stress test results.
- Indicates time to liquidate portfolio to meet redemptions.
- Small-cap funds: Stress test results published monthly since 2024.

### Categorization (SEBI 2017 Circular)

- Each AMC allowed only 1 scheme per category (36 defined categories).
- Categories: Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, Multi Cap, Flexi Cap, ELSS, Value, Contra, Focused, Dividend Yield, Sectoral, Thematic, etc.
- Multi Cap: Mandated minimum 25% each in large, mid, small cap.
- Flexi Cap: No mandated allocation — fund manager discretion.

## 5. Insider Trading Regulations

**Governing law**: SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015 (PIT Regulations)

### Key Definitions

- **Insider**: Any connected person with access to UPSI, or anyone in possession of UPSI.
- **UPSI (Unpublished Price Sensitive Information)**: Financial results, dividends, M&A, changes in key management, material events.
- **Connected person**: Directors, officers, employees, auditors, advisors, relatives of above.

### Trading Window

- **Closure**: Minimum 2 trading days before board meeting for results, and opens 48 hours after results are public.
- Designated persons must not trade during closed window.
- Pre-clearance required for trades above threshold (company-defined, typically Rs 10 lakh).

### Contra-Trade Restrictions

- Designated persons cannot execute opposite transaction (buy after sell or vice versa) within **6 months**.
- Violation: Profit disgorged to SEBI Investor Protection Fund.

### Code of Conduct

- Listed companies must have: Code of Conduct for insiders, Code of Fair Disclosure.
- Designated persons must disclose holdings: Initial (within 7 days of becoming designated), continual (within 2 trading days if trade exceeds Rs 10 lakh in a quarter), annual (by March 31).

### Penalties

- Monetary penalty: Up to Rs 25 crore or 3x profit made (whichever higher).
- Imprisonment: Up to 10 years for willful violation.
- Disgorgement of unfair gains.
- Debarment from securities market.
