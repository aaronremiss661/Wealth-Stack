# Income Tax Rules for Investments — FY 2025-26

> Income Tax Act, 1961 — Investment-relevant provisions.
> Applicable: FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27). Last updated: 2026-03-25.

---

## 1. Capital Gains Taxation (Post July 2024 Union Budget)

### Equity & Equity Mutual Funds

| Parameter | STCG | LTCG |
|-----------|------|------|
| Holding period threshold | ≤ 12 months | > 12 months |
| Tax rate | **20%** (Section 111A) | **12.5%** (Section 112A) |
| Exemption | None | Rs 1,25,000 per financial year |
| Applicable to | Listed equity, equity MF (≥65% equity), business trust units | Same |
| STT requirement | STT must be paid on acquisition (purchase) and/or transfer (sale) | Same |
| Surcharge cap | Normal surcharge applicable | Surcharge capped at 15% (no super-rich surcharge) |

### Debt Instruments & Gold

| Instrument | Holding for LTCG | LTCG Rate | STCG Rate |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|-----------|
| Listed bonds/debentures | > 12 months | 12.5% (no indexation) | Slab rate |
| Unlisted bonds | > 24 months | 12.5% (no indexation) | Slab rate |
| Gold (physical) | > 24 months | 12.5% (no indexation) | Slab rate |
| Gold ETF / Gold MF | > 12 months (listed) | 12.5% (no indexation) | Slab rate |
| Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB) | > 12 months if sold on exchange | 12.5% — BUT exempt at maturity (8 years) | Slab rate |
| **Debt Mutual Funds** (post April 2023) | **No LTCG benefit** | **Slab rate regardless of holding period** | **Slab rate** |

**Critical note on Debt MFs**: For units purchased on or after 1 April 2023, gains from debt mutual funds (equity exposure <35%) are **always taxed at slab rate** with no indexation benefit, irrespective of holding period. This was introduced via Finance Act 2023 (Section 50AA).

### Real Estate / Property

| Parameter | Value (Post July 2024) |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Holding period for LTCG | > 24 months |
| LTCG rate | **12.5% without indexation** |
| STCG rate | Slab rate |
| Section 54 exemption | Reinvest in 1 residential house within 1 year before / 2 years after (or construct within 3 years) |
| Section 54EC | Invest up to Rs 50 lakh in NHAI/REC bonds within 6 months, 5-year lock-in |
| TDS on property sale (Section 194-IA) | 1% of sale consideration if > Rs 50 lakh |

**Indexation removal**: The July 2024 budget removed indexation for all asset classes. The LTCG rate was reduced from 20% (with indexation) to 12.5% (without indexation). For properties acquired before 23 July 2024, taxpayers can choose the lower of: (a) 20% with indexation, or (b) 12.5% without indexation.

## 2. Tax Deductions — Old Regime

> Note: These deductions are available **only under the Old Tax Regime** unless specifically noted.

### Section 80C — Rs 1,50,000 Limit (Aggregate)

| Instrument | Lock-in | Expected Return | Notes |
|-----------|---------|----------------|-------|
| PPF (Public Provident Fund) | 15 years | 7.1% (Q1 FY26) | EEE — Exempt at all stages |
| ELSS (Equity Linked Savings) | 3 years | Market-linked | Shortest lock-in under 80C; LTCG taxable above 1.25L |
| EPF (Employee Provident Fund) | Till retirement/5yr | 8.25% (FY25) | Employer contribution in 80C; interest on >Rs 2.5L taxable |
| NSC (National Savings Certificate) | 5 years | 7.7% | Interest accrued counts as 80C reinvestment |
| SCSS (Senior Citizens Savings) | 5 years | 8.2% | Max Rs 30 lakh; interest taxable |
| 5-year Tax Saver FD | 5 years | 6.5-7.5% | Interest taxable at slab |
| Life insurance premium | Policy term | Varies | Premium must be <10% of sum assured for maturity exemption |
| Tuition fees | - | - | For 2 children, actual fees (not donation) |
| Home loan principal repayment | - | - | Part of 80C limit |
| Sukanya Samriddhi | 21 years | 8.2% | EEE; for girl child |

### Section 80D — Health Insurance Premium

| Category | Deduction Limit |
|----------|----------------|
| Self + family (< 60 years) | Rs 25,000 |
| Self + family (≥ 60 years, senior citizen) | Rs 50,000 |
| Parents (< 60 years) | Rs 25,000 (additional) |
| Parents (≥ 60 years) | Rs 50,000 (additional) |
| Maximum possible (self senior + parents senior) | Rs 1,00,000 |
| Preventive health check-up (included in above) | Rs 5,000 |

### Section 80CCD — NPS (National Pension System)

| Sub-section | Deduction | Limit | Regime |
|------------|-----------|-------|--------|
| 80CCD(1) | Employee contribution to NPS | Within 80C limit of Rs 1.5L (10% of salary) | Old regime only |
| **80CCD(1B)** | **Additional NPS contribution** | **Rs 50,000 over and above 80C** | **Old regime only** |
| **80CCD(2)** | **Employer contribution to NPS** | **14% of basic (govt) / 10% of basic (private)** | **Both old & new regime** |

**Key insight**: 80CCD(2) is one of the very few deductions available in the **New Tax Regime**. Employer NPS contribution up to 14% (government) or 10% (private) of basic salary is deductible in both regimes.

### Section 80E — Education Loan Interest

- Deduction on interest paid on education loan for higher education.
- No upper limit on deduction amount.
- Available for 8 years from the year of first repayment.
- Loan must be from recognized financial institution.

### Section 80TTA / 80TTB — Interest Income

| Section | Eligible | Deduction | On |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 80TTA | Non-senior citizens | Rs 10,000 | Savings account interest |
| 80TTB | Senior citizens (≥60) | Rs 50,000 | All deposit interest (savings + FD) |

## 3. New vs Old Tax Regime — FY 2025-26

### New Regime (Default from FY 2023-24 onwards)

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|-------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 4,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs 4,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 8,00,001 – 12,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs 12,00,001 – 16,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs 16,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 20% |
| Rs 20,00,001 – 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above Rs 24,00,000 | 30% |

- **Standard deduction**: Rs 75,000 for salaried/pensioners (increased from Rs 50K in July 2024 budget).
- **Rebate under 87A**: Full rebate if total income ≤ Rs 12,00,000 (effective zero tax up to approximately Rs 12.75L with standard deduction). As per Budget 2025.
- **Deductions NOT available**: 80C, 80D, 80CCD(1B), HRA, LTA, etc.
- **Deductions available**: 80CCD(2) (employer NPS), 80JJAA, Section 24(b) for let-out property.

### Old Regime (Opt-in Required)

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|-------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs 2,50,001 – 5,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 5,00,001 – 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs 10,00,000 | 30% |

- **All deductions available**: 80C (1.5L), 80D (25K-1L), 80CCD(1B) (50K), HRA, LTA, 80E, 80G, etc.
- **Break-even**: Old regime typically beneficial when total deductions exceed approximately Rs 3.75–4.25 lakh.
- **Opt-out**: Salaried employees can switch regimes each year. Business income: one-time irrevocable choice.

### Surcharge (Both Regimes)

| Total Income | Surcharge Rate |
|-------------|---------------|
| Rs 50L – 1Cr | 10% |
| Rs 1Cr – 2Cr | 15% |
| Rs 2Cr – 5Cr | 25% (old) / 25% (new) |
| Above Rs 5Cr | 37% (old) / 25% (new, capped) |

- **New regime surcharge cap**: Maximum 25% surcharge regardless of income level.
- **Capital gains surcharge**: Capped at 15% on LTCG under Section 112A/112.

## 4. Securities Transaction Tax (STT) — FY 2025-26

| Transaction Type | Rate | Payable By |
|-----------------|------|------------|
| Equity delivery — buy | 0.1% | Buyer |
| Equity delivery — sell | 0.1% | Seller |
| Equity intraday — sell | 0.025% | Seller |
| Futures — sell | 0.0125% | Seller |
| Options — sell (on premium) | **0.0625%** (increased from 0.0125% in July 2024) | Seller |
| MF equity — redemption | 0.001% | Seller (AMC) |

**Options STT increase**: The 5x increase in options STT (from 0.0125% to 0.0625%) was introduced in the July 2024 budget to discourage excessive F&O speculation.

## 5. TDS on Securities Income

| Income Type | TDS Rate | Threshold | Section |
|------------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Dividend (resident) | 10% | Above Rs 5,000 per company per year | 194 |
| Dividend (NRI) | 20% | No threshold | 195 |
| Interest on securities (resident) | 10% | Rs 5,000 | 193 |
| LTCG on equity (NRI) | 10% | No threshold | 195 |
| STCG on equity (NRI) | 15% | No threshold | 195 |
| Sale of immovable property | 1% | Above Rs 50 lakh | 194-IA |
| FD interest (resident) | 10% | Rs 40,000 (Rs 50,000 for senior citizens) | 194A |
| FD interest (NRI) | 30% + surcharge | No threshold (NRO); Nil (NRE) | 195 |

- **15G/15H**: Resident with nil tax liability can submit Form 15G (non-senior) / 15H (senior citizen) to avoid TDS.
- **Lower TDS certificate**: Section 197 allows application for lower/nil TDS if actual liability is lower.

## 6. HUF (Hindu Undivided Family) Taxation

- **Separate tax entity**: HUF has its own PAN, own tax slabs (same as individual).
- **Karta**: Manager of HUF, makes investment decisions.
- **HUF income**: Can invest in equity, MF, PMS, real estate — all taxed in HUF's hands.
- **80C**: HUF can claim 80C deduction separately (Rs 1.5L).
- **80D**: HUF can claim health insurance deduction for HUF members.
- **Useful for**: Income splitting — HUF's income not clubbed with Karta's personal income (if genuine HUF).
- **PMS**: HUF can invest in PMS as a non-individual entity (Rs 50L minimum).
- **Partition**: On partition, transferred assets not treated as transfer — no capital gains.

## 7. NPS Tax Treatment at Maturity

| Component | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|--------------|
| 60% lump sum withdrawal | **Tax-free** |
| 40% mandatory annuity purchase | Annuity income **taxable at slab rate** each year |
| Partial withdrawal (25% after 3 years) | Tax-free for specified purposes (education, illness, housing) |
| Death: 100% to nominee | **Tax-free** to nominee |

**Effective tax benefit analysis**: The true benefit of NPS depends on (a) tax saved during accumulation via 80CCD(1B) at marginal rate, (b) returns earned tax-free during accumulation, minus (c) tax paid on annuity income at retirement marginal rate. Net benefit is highest for those in 30% bracket during working years who expect lower bracket at retirement.
