# RBI Monetary Policy & Banking Regulations

> Reserve Bank of India — Monetary policy, banking, NBFC, and lending regulations.
> Applicable: FY 2025-26. Last updated: 2026-03-25.

---

## 1. Current Policy Rates (As of Early 2025)

| Rate | Value | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Repo Rate** | **6.25%** | Rate at which RBI lends to banks overnight (LAF) |
| **Standing Deposit Facility (SDF)** | **6.00%** | Floor rate — banks park excess liquidity with RBI (no collateral) |
| **Marginal Standing Facility (MSF)** | **6.50%** | Ceiling rate — banks borrow from RBI against SLR securities |
| **Bank Rate** | **6.50%** | Rate for long-term lending by RBI to banks |
| **Reverse Repo Rate** | 3.35% | Effectively replaced by SDF; legacy rate |
| **CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)** | **4.00%** | % of NDTL banks must maintain with RBI as cash |
| **SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)** | **18.00%** | % of NDTL banks must maintain in gold/govt securities/cash |

### Rate Corridor (LAF Corridor)

```
MSF (Ceiling):  6.50%
    |
Repo (Policy):  6.25%  <-- Policy rate
    |
SDF (Floor):    6.00%
```

The LAF corridor is **50 bps wide** (25 bps above and below repo). The repo rate is the primary policy tool — changes here cascade to all lending and deposit rates.

### Impact on Investments

| Rate Change | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| Repo cut | Bond prices rise (yields fall), equity positive, FD rates fall with lag |
| Repo hike | Bond prices fall (yields rise), equity negative, FD rates rise with lag |
| CRR cut | More liquidity in banking system, positive for credit growth |
| CRR hike | Reduced liquidity, tightens monetary conditions |

## 2. Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) Framework

### Composition

| Member | Appointment |
|--------|-------------|
| RBI Governor (Chairperson) | Ex-officio |
| RBI Deputy Governor (Monetary Policy) | Ex-officio |
| One RBI Executive Director | Nominated by RBI Central Board |
| 3 External Members | Appointed by Government (Search-cum-Selection Committee) |

- **Total members**: 6
- **Quorum**: 4 members
- **Governor's casting vote**: In case of tie (3-3 split)
- **External member tenure**: 4 years, non-renewable

### Meeting Schedule

- **Frequency**: 6 meetings per year (bi-monthly, approximately every 2 months).
- **Typical months**: February, April, June, August, October, December.
- **Duration**: 3 days per meeting.
- **Resolution publication**: On the last day of the meeting.
- **Minutes**: Published 14 days after the resolution.

### Inflation Targeting Framework

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Target inflation (CPI) | **4%** |
| Tolerance band | **±2%** (i.e., 2% to 6%) |
| Failure trigger | If inflation exceeds 6% or falls below 2% for 3 consecutive quarters |
| Accountability | MPC must write to Government explaining failure + remedial actions |
| Framework review | Every 5 years (last reviewed: 2021, agreement valid through 2026) |

### MPC Stance Options

| Stance | Meaning | Signal |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Accommodative | Bias toward rate cuts; will not raise rates | Dovish |
| Neutral | Open to cut or hike based on data | Balanced |
| Calibrated tightening | Bias toward rate hikes; will not cut rates | Hawkish |
| Withdrawal of accommodation | Transitioning from loose to tighter policy | Moderately hawkish |

## 3. Deposit Insurance — DICGC

**Governing body**: Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (wholly owned RBI subsidiary)

### Coverage

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Coverage amount | **Rs 5,00,000** per depositor per bank |
| Scope | All commercial banks, small finance banks, RRBs, cooperative banks, payment banks |
| Deposits covered | Savings, current, fixed, recurring deposits |
| NOT covered | Deposits of foreign governments, Central/State government, inter-bank deposits |
| Premium | Paid by banks (not depositors) — currently 12 paise per Rs 100 of deposits per annum |

### Key Rules for Depositors

- **Per depositor per bank**: If you have Rs 3L in savings + Rs 4L in FD at same bank, total Rs 7L but only Rs 5L insured.
- **Different banks**: Rs 5L coverage separately at each bank. Strategy: Spread large deposits across banks.
- **Joint accounts**: Treated as separate from individual accounts (additional Rs 5L coverage).
- **Different branches**: Same bank = single coverage regardless of branch.
- **NRE/NRO**: Covered under DICGC.

### Claim Timeline

- DICGC must pay insured deposit within **90 days** of bank liquidation/merger/moratorium being imposed.
- Interim payments possible if full assessment takes longer.

## 4. Priority Sector Lending (PSL)

### Targets

| Bank Type | Overall PSL Target | Agriculture | Micro Enterprises | Weaker Sections |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Domestic commercial banks | 40% of ANBC | 18% (10% small/marginal farmers) | 7.5% | 12% |
| Foreign banks (>20 branches) | 40% of ANBC | 18% | 7.5% | 12% |
| Foreign banks (≤20 branches) | 40% of ANBC | No sub-target | No sub-target | No sub-target |
| Small Finance Banks | 75% of ANBC | 18% | 7.5% | 12% |
| Regional Rural Banks | 75% of ANBC | 18% | 7.5% | 12% |

- **ANBC**: Adjusted Net Bank Credit (net bank credit + investments in non-SLR bonds held in HTM + other specified items).
- **Priority Sector Lending Certificates (PSLCs)**: Banks that fall short can buy PSLCs from banks that exceed targets. Traded on RBI's e-Kuber platform.
- **Investment relevance**: Banks with large PSL shortfall may face RBI penalty (deposit in RIDF at below-market rates). Impacts bank profitability.

## 5. NBFC Regulations — Scale-Based Framework

### Four-Layer Structure (Effective October 2022)

| Layer | Description | Examples | Key Norms |
|-------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Base Layer** | NBFC-ND < Rs 1,000 crore, P2P, AA, NBFC without public funds | Small NBFCs, NBFC-P2P | Basic governance, net owned fund Rs 10 crore |
| **Middle Layer** | NBFC-ND ≥ Rs 1,000 crore, all deposit-taking NBFCs, HFCs, IFCs, IDF | Major HFCs, gold loan NBFCs | Enhanced governance, independent directors, CRO mandatory |
| **Upper Layer** | Top 10 NBFCs by asset size + NBFCs identified by RBI based on parameters | Bajaj Finance, HDFC (pre-merger), SBI Cards | Bank-like regulation, CET1 requirements, LCR (phased) |
| **Top Layer** | NBFCs that pose extreme systemic risk (currently empty — RBI discretion) | None currently | Near-bank-level regulation |

### Key NBFC Norms

| Parameter | Middle Layer | Upper Layer |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Minimum NOF (Net Owned Fund) | Rs 10 crore | Rs 10 crore (may increase) |
| Capital adequacy (CRAR) | 15% | 15% + CET1 of 9% |
| NPA recognition | 90 days overdue | 90 days overdue |
| Provisioning (standard assets) | 0.40% | 0.40% (higher for specific sectors) |
| Independent directors | Min 1/3rd of board | Min 1/2 of board |
| Concentration norms (single borrower) | 20% of Tier I capital | 20% of Tier I capital |
| Concentration norms (group) | 25% of Tier I capital | 25% of Tier I capital |

### NPA Norms

- **Standard asset**: No default.
- **Sub-standard**: NPA for up to 12 months (provisioning: 10%).
- **Doubtful**: NPA for > 12 months (provisioning: 20-100% depending on age).
- **Loss**: Identified as loss by auditor/RBI (provisioning: 100%).
- **90-day rule**: Asset becomes NPA if interest/principal overdue for 90 days (aligned with banks since 2021).

### Fair Practices Code

- Transparent communication of loan terms, interest rates, fees.
- No coercive recovery practices.
- Grievance redressal mechanism mandatory.
- Ombudsman scheme: RBI Integrated Ombudsman covers NBFCs.

## 6. Digital Lending Guidelines (September 2022)

### Framework Overview

| Entity | Role | RBI Oversight |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **RE (Regulated Entity)** | Bank or NBFC that actually lends | Directly regulated |
| **LSP (Lending Service Provider)** | Technology platform that facilitates loan origination | Regulated through RE |
| **DLA (Digital Lending App)** | Mobile/web app used by LSP or RE for lending | Must disclose RE name upfront |

### Key Rules

1. **Disbursement & Repayment**: Must flow directly between RE's account and borrower's bank account. No pass-through via LSP/third party.
2. **KYC**: Must be done by RE (bank/NBFC), not outsourced entirely to LSP.
3. **Disclosure**: Borrower must be informed of: RE name, all-in-cost (APR), cooling-off period, grievance redressal.
4. **Cooling-off/look-up period**: Borrower can exit without penalty within specified period (determined by RE board).
5. **Data**: LSPs cannot store borrower data beyond what's needed for loan servicing. No access to phone contacts/gallery/etc.
6. **Consent**: Explicit borrower consent for each data point collected.

### FLDG (First Loss Default Guarantee) Guidelines (June 2023)

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| FLDG cap | **5% of outstanding loan portfolio** of the batch/cohort |
| Who provides FLDG | LSP or any third party to the RE |
| Form | Cash deposit, FD, or bank guarantee with the RE |
| Invocation timeline | RE must invoke within 120 days of default |
| RE responsibility | RE must continue standard asset classification and provisioning norms irrespective of FLDG |

**Investment relevance**: FLDG norms impact fintech valuations and profitability. 5% cap limits risk-sharing, pushing more credit risk onto RE balance sheets.

## 7. Gold Loan Norms

### Key Parameters

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Maximum LTV (Loan to Value) | **75%** of gold value |
| Gold valuation | Based on 30-day average gold price (domestic) |
| Auction of pledged gold | If borrower defaults — minimum 15 days' notice required |
| NBFC gold loan concentration | RBI has flagged concentration risk — expects diversification |
| End-use monitoring | Not mandated for gold loans (makes them popular for quick liquidity) |
| Interest rate | Deregulated (market-determined); must be transparent (reducing balance or flat rate disclosed) |

### Auction Norms (If Borrower Defaults)

1. Written notice to borrower: Minimum **15 days** before auction.
2. Auction must be transparent — public auction or through recognized platform.
3. Surplus (auction proceeds minus outstanding loan + charges) must be returned to borrower within **10 days**.
4. Cannot auction gold if borrower is in communication and seeking extension (subject to RE policy).

### Regulatory Concerns (Wealth Advisory Context)

- Gold loans from NBFCs are quick (same day) but rates can be 12-24% p.a.
- **Wealth advisory angle**: For HNIs, Loan Against Securities (LAS) at 8-10% from banks is cheaper than gold loans. Gold loans useful for unbanked/underbanked or for speed.
- Bank gold loans generally have lower rates (7-9%) than NBFC gold loans.
- **LTV monitoring**: If gold price drops, lender may ask for top-up or sell gold. Important risk for borrowers in volatile gold markets.

## 9. Upcoming Regulatory Changes to Watch

- **LCR for NBFCs (Upper Layer)**: Phased implementation of Liquidity Coverage Ratio — will impact NBFC funding costs.
- **Expected Credit Loss (ECL) framework**: RBI moving banks and NBFCs from incurred loss to ECL provisioning (similar to IFRS 9). Will increase provisioning requirements.
- **CBDC (Digital Rupee)**: RBI pilot ongoing — e-Rupee for retail and wholesale. May impact payment flows.
- **Connected lending norms**: RBI tightening norms around loans to related parties and group entities.
- **Climate risk disclosure**: RBI exploring climate-related financial disclosure requirements for banks.
