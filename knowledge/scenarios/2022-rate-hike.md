## What Triggered It

### Global Context
1. **Post-COVID Inflation Surge**: Global supply chain disruptions + massive fiscal/monetary stimulus = inflation at 40-year highs in US/EU.
2. **US Fed Tightening**: Fed raised rates from 0% to 5.5% (most aggressive in 40 years). Fed funds rate went from 0.25% to 5.50%.
3. **Russia-Ukraine War** (February 2022): Energy and commodity price spikes. Brent crude hit $130/barrel.
4. **Global Tech Rout**: Nasdaq fell 33% in 2022. Growth-to-value rotation globally.

### Indian Transmission
1. **Imported Inflation**: India's CPI inflation breached 7% (RBI's upper tolerance of 6%) in April 2022, driven by crude oil and edible oil prices.
2. **FII Outflows**: Foreign investors sold a record **$33 billion in FY22-23** (largest ever outflow at that time).
3. **Currency Pressure**: INR depreciated from 74 to 82/$, forcing RBI to deploy ~$70 billion in reserves.
4. **Off-Cycle Rate Hike**: RBI surprised markets with an emergency 40 bps hike on May 4, 2022, signaling urgency.

## Impact on Debt Markets

The 2022 rate hike cycle was particularly painful for debt fund investors:

### Duration Funds (Worst Affected)
- Long-duration gilt funds: NAV fell **5-8%** (mark-to-market losses as yields rose)
- Dynamic bond funds: -3 to -5%
- Many investors who moved from equity to "safe" debt funds during COVID were caught off-guard

### Short-Duration & Liquid Funds
- Relatively unscathed: 0 to -1% impact
- Accrual income eventually offset MTM losses

### Credit Funds
- Spreads widened but no major credit events
- Post-Franklin Templeton (April 2020), credit funds had already cleaned up

### Key Lesson: Duration Risk
Investors learned that "debt = safe" is an oversimplification. Long-duration debt in a rising rate environment can produce equity-like drawdowns. The 10Y government bond yield moving from 6.1% to 7.4% caused ~8% price decline -- significant for a "safe" asset.

## FII vs DII Dynamics

| Flow | FY2022 | FY2023 | Combined |
|------|--------|--------|----------|
| FII (Equity) | -$18 billion | -$15 billion | **-$33 billion** |
| DII (Equity) | +$14 billion | +$12 billion | +$26 billion |
| Net Impact | -$4 billion | -$3 billion | -$7 billion |

The DII (Domestic Institutional Investor) flows -- primarily driven by MF SIPs -- cushioned the market from a deeper fall. This was a structural change from 2008 when FII outflows had no DII counterbalance.

### SIP Flows Stayed Resilient
| Month | SIP Flow (Rs Crore) |
|-------|-------------------|
| April 2022 | 12,040 |
| June 2022 (market bottom) | 12,276 |
| December 2022 | 13,573 |
| March 2023 | 14,276 |

SIP flows actually increased during the correction -- a sign of maturing investor behavior.

## Style & Factor Rotation

The 2022 rate hike cycle caused a significant rotation in investment styles:

| Factor | Performance |
|--------|------------|
| **Value** | Outperformed (+15 to +20% vs growth) |
| **Growth** | Underperformed (-20 to -30%, especially IT/tech) |
| **Quality** | Held up well (-5 to -10%) |
| **Momentum** | Sharp drawdowns (-15 to -20%) |
| **PSU/Dividend Yield** | Significant outperformance (+10 to +25%) |

### Key Winners in 2022-23
- PSU Banks (re-rating on improved asset quality)
- Energy stocks (high commodity prices)
- Capital goods / defense (government capex push)
- Auto (post-COVID demand recovery)

### Key Losers in 2022-23
- IT services (-30%, US recession fears + rupee hedge unwinding)
- New-age tech (Paytm, Zomato, Nykaa fell 50-70% from IPO peaks)
- Small/mid cap growth stocks (-30 to -50%)

## Lessons for Indian Investors

### 1. Duration Risk in Debt Funds Is Real
The 2022 cycle taught investors that long-duration debt funds can lose 5-8% when rates rise sharply. For capital preservation, short-duration or target-maturity funds are more appropriate during rate hike cycles.

### 2. Value Stocks Outperformed Growth
After years of growth outperformance (2014-2021), value stocks dramatically outperformed in 2022-23. PSU banks, energy, and old-economy stocks led the market. Style diversification matters.

### 3. SIP in Small Cap During Correction Gave Outsized Returns by 2024
Small cap indices fell 25-30% during the rate hike cycle. Investors who maintained or increased SIPs in small cap funds during 2022 saw 40-60% returns by end of 2024 as the segment recovered aggressively.

### 4. FD Rates Became Attractive Again
After years of 5% FD rates, the rate hike cycle pushed FD rates to 7-8%. For the first time since 2019, fixed deposits offered meaningfully positive real returns. This provided a genuine alternative for conservative allocation.

### 5. Currency Risk Compounds Equity Risk for NRIs
NRI investors in Indian equities faced a double hit: Nifty down 15% + INR depreciation 10% = ~25% loss in USD terms. Hedging or maintaining a balanced allocation between Indian and international assets matters.

### 6. DII/SIP Flows Are Now a Structural Support
Unlike 2008, the 2022 correction demonstrated that DII flows (driven by monthly SIPs) provide a structural floor for Indian markets. FII selling of $33 billion was largely absorbed by domestic flows. This is a permanent shift in market structure.
